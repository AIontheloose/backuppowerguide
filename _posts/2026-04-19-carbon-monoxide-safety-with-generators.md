# Carbon Monoxide Safety: A Guide for Generator Use at Home

When the power goes out, having a reliable backup generator can be a lifesaver. However, it's crucial to understand the dangers of carbon monoxide (CO) poisoning when using generators. This article will guide you through essential safety measures and recommend products that can help keep your home safe during power outages.

## Understanding Carbon Monoxide Poisoning

Carbon monoxide is an odorless, colorless gas produced by burning fossil fuels like gasoline, propane, natural gas, or diesel. Generators are one of the leading sources of this dangerous gas when used improperly. Even a small amount can be deadly in enclosed spaces, making it imperative to take preventive measures.

## Safety Measures for Generator Use

1. **Proper Placement**: Never use generators inside your home, garage, basement, or any enclosed space. Always place them outdoors on a dry surface at least 20 feet away from windows, doors, and vents to prevent CO from entering the building.

2. **Use Carbon Monoxide Detectors**: Install battery-operated carbon monoxide detectors in key areas of your home, such as near bedrooms. These devices can alert you to dangerous levels of CO before it becomes fatal.

3. **Proper Ventilation**: Ensure good airflow around the generator when using it outdoors. Avoid placing it under a covered patio or porch where fumes might be trapped.

4. **Regular Maintenance**: Keep your generator well-maintained and serviced regularly. A poorly maintained generator can produce more CO than usual, increasing the risk of poisoning.
