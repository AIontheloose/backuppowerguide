# How Much Does a Whole House Generator Cost?

Whole house generators are essential for keeping your home powered during outages. But how much does a whole house generator cost? This guide will break down the typical pricing ranges, factors influencing costs, and help you find the best option for your needs.

## Understanding Whole House Generators

A whole house generator is designed to provide backup power to all electrical systems in your home, including heating, air conditioning, lights, and appliances. They are commonly used during prolonged power outages caused by storms or other natural disasters.

### Factors Influencing Cost

The cost of a whole house generator can vary widely based on several factors:

- **Size**: The capacity of the generator is crucial as it should match your home's electrical needs.
- **Fuel Type**: Generators can run on diesel, propane, or natural gas. Each type has different costs and maintenance requirements.
- **Brand and Quality**: Higher quality generators often come with better warranties and more advanced features.
- **Installation Costs**: Professional installation fees are an additional expense to consider.

## Average Cost of a Whole House Generator

The average cost of a whole house generator ranges from $3,000 to $15,000 or more. Here’s a breakdown:

### Entry-Level Generators
Entry-level models with basic features and capacities suitable for smaller homes can start around $3,000.

### Mid-Range Models
Mid-range generators offer enhanced features such as quieter operation, better fuel efficiency, and longer runtime capabilities. These typically cost between $5,000 to $12,000.

### High-End Generators
High-end models are designed for larger homes or businesses with complex electrical systems. They can have advanced features like automatic start-up and internet connectivity. Prices for these can range from $13,000 to over $15,000.
