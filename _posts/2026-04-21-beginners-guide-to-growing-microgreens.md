## What Are Microgreens?

Microgreens are young plants that have been harvested just after their first true leaves emerge. They can be grown from almost any edible seed and offer a wide range of flavors and textures. Best of all, they are packed with nutrients and can be harvested within 7 to 21 days, making them an excellent option for those who want fresh greens quickly.

## Why Grow Microgreens?

### Flavor and Nutrition

Microgreens contain up to 40 times more nutrients than their full-grown counterparts. They also have a concentrated flavor profile that is much stronger than mature plants. This means you can add small amounts of microgreens to your dishes for a burst of fresh taste without overwhelming the flavors.

### Convenience and Space Efficiency

Growing microgreens requires minimal space, making them perfect for small balconies or windowsills. Additionally, they grow quickly, so you donâ€™t need to wait long before enjoying your harvest.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Microgreens

1. **Prepare Your Workspace**
   - Choose a well-lit area in your kitchen where you can set up your microgreen grow tray.
   - Place the container on top of a grow mat for added light and warmth, especially during power outages.

2. **Soil and Seeds**
   - Use a good quality seed starting mix to fill your containers. Make sure it is sterile to prevent any contamination.
   - Choose organic seeds that are specifically labeled as suitable for microgreen cultivation.

3. **Seeding the Trays**
   - Sow the seeds evenly across the soil surface, ensuring there is enough space between each seed.
   - Cover the seeds with a thin layer of soil and lightly press down to ensure good contact.

4. **Watering and Germination**
   - Water the seeds gently but thoroughly using a spray bottle or misting tool to avoid washing away the seeds.
   - Keep the soil consistently moist until germination occurs, which usually takes 3-7 days depending on the type of seed.
