# Solar Panel Sizing Guide for Home Backup in Australia

Are you looking to ensure your home is prepared for unexpected power outages? A well-designed solar panel system can provide a reliable source of backup power. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to properly size your solar panels for home backup.

## Understanding Your Energy Needs

The first step in sizing your solar panel system is understanding how much energy you need during a power outage. Start by calculating your household’s daily electricity consumption and then determine the number of days you want to cover with your backup system.

### Calculating Daily Consumption

1. **Check Your Electricity Bill**: Look at your recent bills to find out how many kilowatt-hours (kWh) you use per day.
2. **Estimate Usage for Backup Days**: If you plan on covering a full week, multiply the daily consumption by 7.

## Evaluating Local Conditions

The efficiency of solar panels can vary based on local climate and geographic factors. In Australia, the weather patterns significantly influence how much power your system will generate.

### Key Considerations:

- **Solar Irradiance**: This is the amount of sunlight that falls per unit area over a given period. It varies depending on location.
- **Shade Factors**: Obstacles like trees or buildings can reduce the effectiveness of solar panels.
- **Panel Orientation and Angle**: Panels should ideally face north with an angle of 20-35 degrees to maximize energy output.

Proper installation is crucial for the performance of your solar panel system. Ensure you work with a certified installer who can help you choose the right setup based on your specific needs.
