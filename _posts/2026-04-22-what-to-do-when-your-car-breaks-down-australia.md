# What to Do When Your Car Breaks Down in Australia

## Introduction
Every driver hopes their vehicle will never break down. However, accidents can happen unexpectedly, leaving you stranded on the side of a busy road. In this article, we provide practical advice on what to do when your car breaks down in Australia, ensuring both safety and efficiency.

## Stay Safe First
When your car breaks down, the first step is to prioritize your safety. Here’s how:

### Pull Over Safely
If possible, pull over to a safe location off the main traffic lane. Use your hazard lights to warn other drivers of your presence. If you're on a busy road or near an intersection, consider moving closer to the side where traffic is lighter.

### Get Help
If you are alone and need immediate assistance, don’t hesitate to call for help. In Australia, the emergency number is 000, but if it’s a non-emergency situation, you can contact your roadside assistance provider or local transport services.

## Use Your Resources
Before pulling over, make sure you have all necessary resources at hand:

### Emergency Kit
Carrying an emergency kit is crucial. It should include items such as:
- **Jumper Cables:** To help start a stalled car.
- **Flashlight and Batteries:** For visibility in the dark or to signal for help.
- **Reflective Triangle Warning Signs:** To alert other drivers that you are parked on the road.

You can find high-quality roadside emergency kits at [this link](https://www.amazon.com.au/s?k=roadside+emergency+kit&tag=owlno-22).

### Mobile Phone and Charger
Always carry a charged mobile phone. Use it to call for help, take photos of your breakdown (for insurance claims), or update friends and family about your situation.

## Check the Vehicle
Before taking any action, inspect the vehicle:

### Basic Checks
1. **Check the Battery:** Ensure that the battery is not completely dead.
2. **Inspect Fluids:** Look for leaks in oil, coolant, or brake fluid.
3. **Check Tires:** Make sure tires are properly inflated and have sufficient tread.

If you find issues but can’t resolve them yourself, it’s best to call for professional help.

### Contact Assistance
If you’ve tried basic checks without success, contact a roadside assistance service like AA Australia or RACQ (Royal Automobile Club of Queensland).

## Legal Considerations
In Australia, there are specific laws and regulations regarding how to handle a breakdown. For instance:

- **Emergency Lane Use:** You must use the emergency lane if available.
- **Visibility Requirements:** Your vehicle should have working lights during low visibility conditions.

### Stay Calm
While it’s natural to feel anxious when your car breaks down, staying calm helps you think more clearly and respond effectively. Take deep breaths and follow the steps outlined above.

## Conclusion
Dealing with a car breakdown can be stressful, but by following these guidelines, you can ensure both safety and efficiency. Always keep an emergency kit in your vehicle and stay informed about local laws and resources. With preparation and a calm mindset, you’ll be better equipped to handle any situation that may arise on the road.
