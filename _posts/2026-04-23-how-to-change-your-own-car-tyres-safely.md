## Introduction

Maintaining your vehicle is an essential part of owning a car, especially when it comes to tyre safety. Changing your own car tyres may seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and some basic guidance, you can handle it yourself safely. This article will walk you through the process of changing your car tyres, providing practical tips and recommendations for Australian drivers.

## Tools You Will Need

Before you begin, ensure you have all the necessary tools:

- **Jack:** A hydraulic jack is essential to lift your vehicle off the ground. [Choose a reliable jack](https://www.amazon.com.au/s?k=car+jack&tag=owlno-22) that fits your car’s make and model.
- **Wrench or Spanner Set:** You will need this to loosen and remove the wheel nuts. A set that includes different sizes is recommended.
- **Tyre Pressure Gauge:** This tool helps you check if the tyre pressure is correct before and after changing tyres. [Get a high-quality gauge](https://www.amazon.com.au/s?k=tyre+pressure+gauge&tag=owlno-22) to ensure accurate readings.
- **Tie Rods or Lug Wrench:** These are used to secure the wheel nuts after you have replaced the tyre.

## Step-by-Step Guide

### 1. Park Your Vehicle Safely
Park your car on a flat, hard surface away from traffic and other obstructions. Engage the handbrake and place chocks under the opposite wheels to prevent it from moving during the process.

### 2. Consult Your Vehicle’s Manual
Check your vehicle manual for specific instructions related to changing tyres, as different vehicles have varying requirements.

### 3. Loosen the Wheel Nuts
Using a wrench or spanner set, loosen the wheel nuts by turning them counterclockwise. Do not remove them yet; you will need to do this after jacking up your car.

### 4. Jack Up Your Vehicle
Position the jack under the vehicle at the correct point as specified in your manual (often marked on the underside of the bonnet). Slowly raise the vehicle until the tyre is off the ground.

### 5. Remove the Wheel Nuts and Tyre
With the tyre no longer touching the ground, remove the wheel nuts completely and carefully pull the old tyre towards you to release it from its mounting position.

### 6. Mount the New Tyre
Place the new tyre onto the hub until the rim is visible underneath. Align the valve stem with the wheel hub opening.

### 7. Tighten the Wheel Nuts
Lower the vehicle gently using the jack, and tighten the wheel nuts by hand first. Use a torque wrench to ensure they are tightened evenly according to your car’s specifications.

### 8. Lower the Vehicle Completely
Once all the wheel nuts are tight, use the jack to lower the vehicle completely back onto the ground.

### 9. Check the Tyre Pressure
Using your tyre pressure gauge, check that both the old and new tyres have the correct inflation levels as specified in your car’s manual or on the side of the tyre itself.

## Tips for Safety

- **Wear Protective Clothing:** Always wear gloves and protective clothing to avoid injury.
- **Stay Away from Traffic:** Ensure you are working in a safe area away from traffic. If possible, use wheel chocks and warning triangles to alert other drivers.
- **Use Caution with the Jack:** Never put your hand between the tyre and jack or under the vehicle. The jack should only be used on level ground.
