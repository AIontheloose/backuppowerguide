# Introduction to Solar Incentives and Tax Credits in Australia

As we move into 2026, the Australian government continues to support renewable energy through various incentives and tax credits. This year brings a range of updates and changes that can significantly reduce the cost of installing a solar power system for your home or business.

## Understanding Solar Incentives

### The Small-Scale Renewable Energy Scheme (SRES)

The SRES, now known as the Small-Scale Technology Certificates (STCs), provides financial assistance through the purchase of certificates. These certificates are awarded to renewable energy systems and can be sold by installers at the time of installation. Buyers then receive a credit based on these certificates.

### The Renewable Energy Target (RET)

The RET aims to increase Australia’s use of renewable energy sources, including solar power, to help meet national targets. While not directly providing financial incentives for individual households, it plays an essential role in driving down costs and supporting the growth of the industry.

## Key Changes for 2026

### STC Price Reductions

Starting from January 1, 2026, there has been a significant reduction in the price of STCs. This means that homeowners can get more value out of their STC credits when purchasing a solar system, potentially lowering the upfront cost.

### Eligibility and Installation Costs

To qualify for these incentives, your solar panel system must meet certain technical requirements and be installed by an accredited installer. The cost savings from the reduced STC prices will vary depending on the size and type of system you install.
