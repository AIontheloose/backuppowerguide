## Introduction

Raised garden beds are a game‑changer for Australian home gardeners. They give you better soil control, easier access, and a compact footprint that’s perfect for balconies, patios or any corner of your yard. Whether you’re a seasoned horticulturist or a green‑thumb beginner, this step‑by‑step guide will walk you through designing, building, and maintaining a raised bed that thrives in the Australian climate.

---

## 1. Planning Your Bed

### Size matters

- **Standard size**: 120 cm × 60 cm (4 ft × 2 ft) is a good starting point. It’s large enough to plant a variety of veggies but small enough to be managed from one side.
- **Depth**: Aim for 30–45 cm (12–18 in) to allow for a healthy root zone. In drier regions you might go deeper (up to 60 cm) to store more moisture.

### Location

- **Sunlight**: Pick a spot that receives at least 6 hours of direct sun per day.
- **Drainage**: Avoid low‑lying areas that collect rainwater. If you’re in a flood‑prone zone, consider elevating the bed on a concrete base.

### Materials

| Material | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| **Cedar** | Naturally rot‑resistant, pleasant scent | More expensive |
| **Cypress** | Durable, affordable | Slightly less fragrant |
| **Reclaimed timber** | Eco‑friendly, unique look | Requires extra prep |
| **Composite boards** | Low maintenance, long‑lasting | Can be pricey |

**Tip:** In Queensland, you might use *Tamarind* timber; in Western Australia, *Sheoak* works well.

## 3. Step‑by‑Step Construction

### Step 1: Design & Cut

1. **Sketch** your bed on paper, noting length, width, and depth.
2. **Mark** the timber pieces. For a 120 cm × 60 cm bed, you’ll need:
   - Two long sides: 120 cm each
   - Two short sides: 60 cm each
   - Optional: a middle support beam if you’re building a double‑layered bed.

3. **Cut** the boards to size. If you’re using 4 cm thick timber, each side will be 4 cm thick.

### Step 2: Build the Frame

1. **Lay out** the long sides on a flat surface.
2. **Attach** the short sides using screws or nails. Pre‑drill holes to avoid splitting the wood.
3. **Reinforce** corners with metal brackets for extra stability.
