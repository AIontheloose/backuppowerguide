Australia's natural beauty is best experienced on the open road, and Melbourne serves as the perfect launching point for countless unforgettable journeys. Whether you're after pristine beaches, rugged mountain ranges, or world-class wine regions, the road from Melbourne offers something for every type of traveller. From the moment you hit the highway, you're embarking on an adventure that showcases the diverse landscapes and rich culture that make Australia so special.

## The Great Ocean Road: Coastal Splendour

No list of Melbourne road trips would be complete without the legendary Great Ocean Road. This 243-kilometre coastal drive takes you from the Port Campbell National Park to the Bellarine Peninsula, offering some of the most spectacular ocean views in the country. The journey typically takes 3-4 hours, depending on how many stops you make.

The highlights include the iconic Twelve Apostles, Loch Ard Gorge, and the dramatic coastal cliffs that have been carved by millions of years of ocean erosion. Don't miss the chance to visit the historic Lorne, where you can enjoy a spot of beachcombing and perhaps catch a sunset over the ocean. For those who love a good picnic, the beaches around the road offer plenty of scenic spots to stop and enjoy your lunch.

## The Alpine Way: Mountain Majesty

For those seeking a more rugged adventure, the Alpine Way offers an incredible journey through the Australian Alps. Starting from Melbourne's eastern suburbs, this route takes you through the scenic Victorian Alps, with stops at the charming towns of Bright and Beechworth. The drive itself takes about 4-5 hours, but you'll want to factor in time for the numerous attractions along the way.

The Alpine Way passes through some of Australia's most beautiful mountain scenery, with opportunities for hiking, skiing, and exploring ancient forests. Don't miss the chance to visit the historic town of Beechworth, where you can sample some of the region's finest wines and enjoy the town's colonial architecture. The route also offers spectacular views of the Victorian Alps, especially during the spring months when the wildflowers are in bloom.

## The Great Western Highway: Historic Routes

The Great Western Highway is one of Australia's most historic road trips, stretching from Melbourne to the town of Ballarat. This 200-kilometre journey takes you through some of Victoria's most picturesque countryside, passing through historic towns like Sunbury and Macedon. The drive typically takes 2-3 hours, but the journey is filled with stops that make it a worthwhile adventure.

The highway offers a unique glimpse into Australia's colonial past, with numerous historic sites and museums along the way. The town of Macedon is particularly worth a stop, with its beautiful gardens and historic buildings. The route also passes through some of the most beautiful farmland in Victoria, offering stunning views of the countryside and opportunities for some excellent local food experiences.

## The Yarra Valley Wine Route: Culinary Delights

For wine lovers, the Yarra Valley Wine Route offers an incredible journey through one of Australia's most celebrated wine regions. Starting from Melbourne's eastern suburbs, this route takes you through the scenic Yarra Valley, where you'll discover world-class wineries and cellar doors. The drive typically takes 1-2 hours, depending on how many stops you make.

The Yarra Valley is home to some of Australia's finest wineries, including the famous Domaine Chandon and the historic Yeringberg. The region also offers excellent dining experiences, with numerous restaurants and cafes serving local produce. The area is particularly beautiful during the spring and autumn months, when the changing seasons create spectacular backdrops for your wine tasting adventures.

## The Otway National Park: Forest and Coast

The journey from Melbourne to the Otway National Park offers a unique blend of forest exploration and coastal beauty. This route takes you through the lush rainforests of the Otway Range, with stops at the famous Otway Fly, where you can experience the thrill of flying through the treetops. The drive typically takes 2-3 hours, with numerous stops along the way.

The Otway National Park is home to some of Australia's most beautiful rainforest, with opportunities for hiking, wildlife spotting, and photography. The park also features some of the most beautiful coastal scenery in Victoria, with dramatic cliffs and pristine beaches. The town of Port Campbell, located at the edge of the park, offers excellent accommodation options and dining experiences.

## The Grampians National Park: Rugged Beauty

For those seeking a more challenging adventure, the journey to the Grampians National Park offers some of Australia's most rugged and beautiful landscapes. This 300-kilometre drive from Melbourne takes you through the heart of the Grampians, with stops at the famous Mount Buffalo and the stunning Pinnacle Rock. The journey typically takes 4-5 hours, but the scenery makes every moment worthwhile.
