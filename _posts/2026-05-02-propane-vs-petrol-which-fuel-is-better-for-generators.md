## Cost Considerations

When examining the total cost of ownership, both fuel types present distinct advantages and disadvantages:

### Fuel Prices (Per Litre in Australia)

| Fuel Type | Price Range (AUD/L) |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Propane   | $1.20 – $1.80       |
| Petrol    | $1.80 – $2.20       |

While petrol is generally more expensive per litre, **propane generators often deliver better fuel efficiency**, meaning you might use less fuel overall. For example, a 5kW propane generator can run for up to **12 hours on a 200L tank**, while a petrol generator may need refueling every 4–6 hours under similar load.

## Safety and Storage

Safety considerations play a crucial role in choosing between these two fuel types.

### Petrol Safety

- Highly flammable and volatile.
- Requires careful handling and storage away from heat sources.
- Risk of fire or explosion makes storage particularly important during **bushfire seasons**.
- Must be rotated regularly to prevent fuel degradation.

### Propane Safety

- Designed with multiple safety features including pressure relief valves and leak detection systems.
- Heavier than air, so any leaks settle to the ground rather than rise and ignite.
- More flexible in placement due to safer tank designs.

> 🚨 *In bushfire-prone areas, propane systems are generally preferred due to their lower fire risk.*

## Maintenance Requirements

Maintenance needs differ significantly between propane and petrol generators:

### Petrol Generator Maintenance

- Regular oil changes (every 50–100 hours).
- Spark plug replacements.
- Fuel system cleaning to prevent gum and varnish buildup.
- Periodic carburetor adjustments.

### Propane Generator Maintenance

- Less frequent oil changes.
- No gum or carbon deposits from clean-burning propane.
- Regular inspection of gas connections and regulators.
- Annual service by a qualified technician.

> ✅ *Propane generators require less maintenance and offer longer intervals between services.*

## Environmental Impact

From an environmental perspective, **propane generates fewer emissions** compared to petrol:

- Burns cleaner, producing less carbon monoxide and other harmful pollutants.
- A byproduct of natural gas processing and petroleum refining, making it a more efficient use of existing resources.
- Better suited for urban environments where air quality is a concern.

> 🌿 *Propane is considered a more environmentally friendly option for residential backup power.*

## Australian Considerations

When choosing between propane and petrol generators for Australian homes, several location-specific factors should be considered:

### Bushfire Zones

- Propane systems are generally more suitable due to their lower fire risk.
- Propane tanks are designed with safety features that reduce ignition risks.

### Remote Locations

- Propane delivery services are widely available across Australia.
- Easier to maintain consistent fuel supplies in rural areas.

### Power Outage Frequency

- In areas with frequent, extended outages, the longer runtime of propane generators can provide significant advantages.

Both propane and petrol generators offer viable solutions for Australian home backup power needs. Propane generators generally provide:
