## 2. Switch to LED Lighting

**How many bulbs?**  
LED bulbs use **up to 80% less energy** than incandescent ones and last **25 times longer**.

**Savings example:**  
A typical 60-watt incandescent bulb can be replaced with a 6-watt LED bulb that gives the same brightness. For a household with 20 light fixtures, switching to LED could save **$150/year**.

**Free upgrades available:**  
Many Australian energy retailers offer free LED bulb giveaways as part of their energy efficiency programs. Check with your provider or visit [Energy.gov.au](https://energy.gov.au) to see if you qualify.

## 3. Optimise Your Home's Insulation and Ventilation

### Insulation  
Proper insulation keeps your home cooler in summer and warmer in winter, reducing reliance on air conditioning and heating.

**Key areas to focus on:**  
- Attic and wall insulation  
- Weather stripping around doors and windows  
- Insulated curtains and blinds  

### Ventilation  
Natural ventilation can reduce cooling costs by up to **20%**.

**Summer tips:**  
- Close curtains during the day  
- Open windows at night for cross-ventilation  
- Use ceiling fans to circulate air  

💡 *Aussie slang alert:* “Mate, if you’re not using a fan or proper insulation, you’re just throwing money at the power company!”

## 4. Choose the Right Time to Use High-Energy Appliances

**Peak vs Off-Peak Rates**  
In Australia, electricity costs **2–3 times more** during peak hours (6–9 AM and 6–9 PM).

**Smart scheduling tips:**  
- Run dishwashers and washing machines in the evening  
- Charge electric vehicles during cheaper periods  
- Use electric heaters during off-peak times  

**Time-of-Use Tariffs**  
Many energy providers now offer smart meters that show real-time pricing. Some smart home systems can automatically schedule appliance usage based on rates – making it effortless to save money.

## 5. Monitor Your Energy Usage with Smart Technology

**Real-time data is key.**  
Smart meters and energy monitoring apps help you identify energy hogs and adjust consumption habits.

**Recommended tools:**  
- **Smart thermostats** (e.g., Nest, Ecobee)  
- **Energy monitoring apps** (e.g., Sense, MyEnergi)  
- **Smart plugs** with detailed power tracking  

**Action checklist:**  
- [ ] Install a smart meter or energy monitor  
- [ ] Set alerts for unusual usage  
- [ ] Review monthly bills for trends  

💡 *Bonus tip:* If your smart home system supports it, schedule your appliances to run during off-peak hours for maximum savings.

## Take Advantage of Government Incentives

Australia offers various programs to help reduce energy costs:

- **Free energy audits** for eligible households  
- **Rebates for energy-efficient appliances**  
- **Solar panel incentives** through ARENA  
- **Home insulation grants** in certain regions  

**Where to start:**  
Visit your state government website or the [Australian Government’s energy efficiency portal](https://energy.gov.au) to check your eligibility.

## Making Long-Term Savings

The most effective approach combines several strategies:

1. Start with simple changes like LED bulbs and smart power strips  
2. Gradually invest in insulation and smart home tech  
3. Monitor usage with real-time tools  

💡 *Remember:* Every small change adds up. With smart planning and the right tools, Australian households can reduce their electricity bills by **20–40%** while staying comfortable and sustainable.
