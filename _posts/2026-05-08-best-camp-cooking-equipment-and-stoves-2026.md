## The State of the Market in 2026

The outdoor cooking landscape has matured significantly, driven by stricter safety standards and a consumer base that demands versatility without bulk. Australian Standards AS/NZS 5044 now governs nearly all portable gas appliances sold through major retailers like BCF, Anaconda, and Repco, ensuring that pressure regulators, valve integrity, and flame failure devices meet rigorous benchmarks. Dual-fuel capability has become the industry baseline, with most reputable manufacturers engineering burners to accept both standard household propane bottles (which are widely available in regional supermarkets) and lightweight camping-specific butane canisters. This hybrid approach eliminates the logistical panic of running dry in remote corridors like the Nullarbor or the Daintree, allowing you to switch fuel sources without carrying redundant hardware.

Sustainability and efficiency have also reshaped purchasing behaviour. Modern piezo ignition systems are now standard across mid-to-high-tier models, reducing reliance on disposable lighters and minimising wind-related ignition failures. While solar cooking remains a niche pursuit in Australia due to variable cloud cover and intense UV interference, hybrid solar-gas ovens have gained traction among caravanners who want to supplement daytime cooking without draining vehicle batteries. The market has also shifted toward corrosion-resistant alloys and powder-coated finishes, directly addressing the rapid rust degradation that plagued aluminium gear in coastal and high-humidity environments just five years ago.

## How We Tested and Selected the Top Gear

Our evaluation process combined laboratory measurements with real-world Australian conditions. We measured heat output using calibrated gas flow meters, timed boil cycles with one litre of tap water, and recorded fuel consumption rates across continuous 20-minute runs. Wind resistance was assessed using a calibrated fan array simulating coastal gales up to 45 km/h, while stability was tested on uneven terrain, wet grass, and loose gravel. We prioritised models that met DOT/NPAD compliance for portable gas cylinders, featured replaceable ignition components, and offered clear warranty terms through authorised Australian distributors. Testing locations included the wind-scoured cliffs of Wilsons Promontory, the high-altitude camps of Mount Field National Park, and the dust-choked outback tracks near Uluru, ensuring that every recommendation performs where Australian camping actually happens.

## Pro Tips for Australian Conditions

**Pro Tip:** Always carry a spare ignition source. While piezo systems are reliable, high humidity and salt spray can occasionally cause minor electrode corrosion. A simple ferro rod or windproof lighter should be kept in a dry bag, especially when camping along the east coast or in tropical north Queensland.

**Pro Tip:** In regions with active bushfire bans, check your state’s fire danger rating before bringing any open-flame cooking equipment into national parks. Many Australian states now require enclosed, regulator-controlled gas stoves rather than open-jet burners during high-risk periods. Always verify local regulations via your state’s emergency services website before departure.

**Pro Tip:** If you frequently camp in coastal or high-humidity zones, wipe down your burner after each use with a dry cloth and apply a thin layer of food-safe mineral oil to metal joints. This simple maintenance step prevents salt-induced pitting and extends the life of your ignition components significantly.
