### The Financial and Structural Landscape

To make an informed decision, consumers must first grasp the financial realities of Australia's mobile market. As of Q1 2026, Android holds approximately 55 per cent of the Australian smartphone market, while iOS accounts for roughly 45 per cent, according to the Australian Communications & Media Authority (ACMA). This split reflects a diverse consumer base that values both ecosystem integration and hardware flexibility.

The average retail price of a new flagship phone in Australia sits at AUD 1,250, though actual costs vary significantly. For instance, the iPhone 15 Pro is priced at $1,250, the Samsung Galaxy S24 Ultra comes in at $1,350, and the Google Pixel 8 Pro retails at $1,200. It is crucial to remember that all Australian retail prices include a 10 per cent Goods and Services Tax (GST). Furthermore, import duties play a subtle but measurable role in secondary-market valuations: iPhones attract a 5 per cent import duty, whereas Android devices face a 10 per cent duty. This duty differential often explains why used Android flagships depreciate faster than their iOS counterparts, making second-hand Androids a surprisingly cost-effective entry point for budget-conscious buyers.
