## What to Look For: The 5‑Point Buying Guide

Navigating the market can be overwhelming. Here's my expanded guide to picking the right tool for your Aussie business.

1.  **Local Data Residency**  
    *Why it matters*: 61% of Australian firms require AI-generated data to stay on Australian servers. Check that the provider offers a local endpoint (e.g., Azure AI Services Australia) and that your data never leaves the country unless you sign a Data Transfer Agreement. Look for the 'Australian Data Residency' badge on provider websites.

2.  **Token Efficiency & Cost Control**  
    *Why it matters*: Token-based billing can quickly exceed budgets. Set a monthly token cap (e.g., 800 k tokens for GPT-4o) and monitor usage via the provider's dashboard. Look for tools that offer prompt-template optimisation to reduce token consumption by ~30%. Consider tools available at JB Hi-Fi's digital software store for bundled subscriptions.

3.  **Compliance & Explainability**  
    *Why it matters*: The AI Act requires explainability dashboards for high-impact AI. Verify that the tool publishes AS/NZS ISO 27001 certificates and offers built-in risk-assessment reports. The Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) recommends tools that generate audit logs automatically.

4.  **Native Integration**  
    *Why it matters*: 82% of Australian users need seamless integration with Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace. A tool that plugs into your existing suite saves time and reduces friction. Ensure the integration supports Australian date formats and currency settings out of the box.

5.  **Multimodal Capabilities**  
    *Why it matters*: Design-heavy teams benefit from image-to-text and video summarisation. Gemini Pro's 10 MB image support can auto-generate alt-text, saving 15 minutes per image. This is vital for meeting Web Accessibility Standards (WCAG) required by many government tenders.

> **Pro Tip:** Before signing up, run a quick test using a dummy dataset to verify the 'Explainability Dashboard' required by the AI Act. If the tool can't show you exactly how it reached a decision for high-risk tasks, walk away. Test the tool with Australian slang (e.g., "G'day mate, chuck a slugger together") to ensure the language model understands local context.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Do I need to pay GST on AI tool subscriptions?**  
*A1:* Yes, all software subscriptions are subject to 9.5% GST under Australian tax law. However, many providers now offer GST-included pricing tiers for Australian businesses, simplifying bookkeeping and ensuring your ATO reporting is accurate. Always check the checkout page for 'GST Included' labels to avoid surprises, and keep receipts for claims through your accountant.

**Q2: Can I use a free AI tool for my small business?**  
*A2:* Free tiers exist (e.g., Claude Free), but they often lack local data residency, native integration, or token-efficient prompts. For compliance-heavy SMEs, a paid plan is usually worth the investment to avoid regulatory breaches. Free tools may also store data overseas, violating the Data Sovereignty Act, so weigh the cost of potential fines against the subscription fee.

**Q3: How do I monitor my token usage to stay within budget?**  
*A3:* Most providers offer real-time dashboards where you can track token consumption and set alerts. You can configure thresholds to notify you when you're 80% of your budget, allowing you to optimise prompts or scale down usage before overruns occur. Additionally, some Australian retailers like Harvey Norman offer budgeting software bundles that integrate with these dashboards for holistic financial management.

**Q4: Are these tools safe for sensitive data like customer emails?**  
*A4:* Yes, if you use local endpoints and comply with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). Ensure your chosen tool publishes ISO 27001 certifications and offers end-to-end encryption. Always conduct a privacy impact assessment before uploading sensitive data, and verify that the provider has a clear data deletion policy compliant with OAIC guidelines.
