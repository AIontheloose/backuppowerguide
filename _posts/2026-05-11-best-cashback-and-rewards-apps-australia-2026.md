## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Do I need to install a browser extension to use these apps?
Most cashback apps offer a browser extension that automatically tracks eligible purchases and applies your cashback at checkout, but it isn’t strictly mandatory. You can also use the mobile app to scan QR codes, manually enter the merchant’s URL, or activate cashback through the app’s native browser. Extensions remain the most convenient option for desktop shoppers, as they eliminate the risk of forgotten activation and provide real-time rate visibility before you commit to a purchase.

### 2. How often can I withdraw my cashback?
All four leading apps have a **minimum payout threshold of AUD 50**, and once you hit that amount, you can choose to receive a bank transfer, PayPal, or a gift card, depending on the platform’s available options. Processing times typically range from three to ten business days, with bank transfers taking slightly longer than digital wallets. Some apps allow instant withdrawal once the threshold is met, but this varies by payout method and your account verification status.

### 3. Are there any hidden fees or taxes on cashback earnings?
Cashback is generally considered a rebate or discount rather than assessable income, so it is not taxed by the Australian Taxation Office for everyday consumers. However, if you earn a large sum through commercial use or affiliate activities, it may be reported as a taxable event for your accountant. Always keep records of your earnings, retain transaction confirmations, and consult a qualified tax professional if your usage crosses into business territory or if you’re claiming deductions for related expenses.

### 4. Can I use multiple cashback apps simultaneously?
Yes, you can install and use multiple apps, but many merchants only allow one cashback app per transaction due to affiliate network restrictions. It’s best to check the app’s FAQ or customer support for guidance on which app to use for specific retailers, as some tracks may override others if activated in the wrong order. A practical approach is to designate one primary app for everyday spend and keep a secondary app handy for category-specific purchases where it offers a demonstrably higher rate.
