### What to Look For in a Dual-Fuel Generator

Before committing to a purchase, prospective buyers must evaluate several non-negotiable criteria. First and foremost is AS/NZS compliance. All legitimate units sold in Australia must meet AS/NZS 4859.1 for portable generator safety and performance, and they must integrate properly with AS/NZS 3000 wiring rules to ensure safe connection to domestic circuits. Look for models that include a certified transfer switch or are explicitly rated for hardwiring, as this prevents backfeeding and protects utility workers during outages. Retailers like Officeworks and Mitre 10 now stock only AS/NZS-compliant models, making verification straightforward.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I safely operate a dual-fuel generator inside my home or garage?**
A: Absolutely not. Dual-fuel generators produce carbon monoxide, a lethal odourless gas. They must always be operated outdoors, at least 3 metres away from windows, doors, and vents, regardless of fuel type.

**Q: How does propane runtime compare to petrol at a 5 kW load?**
A: Propane typically burns cleaner and slightly slower than petrol. At a 50% load, a standard 5 kW unit will run approximately 8–10 hours on a standard 9 kg LPG cylinder, whereas the same load on petrol may yield 7–9 hours depending on tank size and engine tuning. Propane also requires less seasonal carburettor adjustment in humid Australian climates and leaves minimal residue in the fuel system.

**Q: Is a transfer switch legally required in Australia?**
A: Yes. Under AS/NZS 3000 (Wiring Rules) and state-based electrical safety regulations, any permanent or semi-permanent connection between a generator and your home’s electrical panel must use a certified interlock kit or transfer switch. Direct extension cord feeding into wall outlets is illegal and poses severe backfeeding risks to network technicians.

**Q: Do I need to change the oil before storing a generator for months?**
A: Yes. Before long-term storage, run the unit until it reaches operating temperature, then drain the old oil and refill with the manufacturer-specified grade. Add a fuel stabiliser to the petrol tank or switch to propane, which does not degrade over time and eliminates varnish buildup in the carburettor.

**Q: Will a 5 kW dual-fuel generator run my entire house during an outage?**
A: It depends on your essential load. A 5 kW continuous output can reliably power a fridge, freezer, LED lighting, Wi-Fi router, and one small air conditioner simultaneously. However, it will not run electric stoves, hot water systems, or multiple high-draw appliances at once. Always calculate your critical load before purchasing.
