## Pro Tips for Faster Recovery

- **Hydrate with electrolytes**: Plain water isn’t always enough when you’re running a temperature. Add a pinch of sea salt and lemon to your

water to replenish lost minerals and support cellular hydration.
- **Prioritise sleep and rest**: Your immune system does its heaviest lifting while you sleep. Aim for 8–10 hours nightly and avoid screen time before bed to improve recovery quality.
- **Steam or humidify your space**: Dry air worsens congestion and throat irritation. Use a cool-mist humidifier or take warm showers to keep mucous membranes moist and ease breathing.
- **Know when to seek medical help**: If symptoms last longer than 10 days, you develop a high fever over 39°C, experience shortness of breath, or notice chest pain, consult a GP or visit an urgent care clinic promptly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I take multiple cold and flu medications at once?**
A: Not without caution. Many OTC products contain overlapping active ingredients like acetaminophen or dextromethorphan. Taking them together can lead to dangerous overdoses. Always check labels carefully and consult a pharmacist if you’re combining treatments.

**Q: Is Manuka honey safe for children?**
A: Manuka honey is generally safe for children over one year old due to its natural antibacterial properties. However, never give honey to infants under 12 months because of the risk of infant botulism.

**Q: How long does it take for zinc lozenges to work?**
A: Zinc is most effective when started within 24 hours of symptom onset. Consistent dosing every 2–3 hours while awake can help shorten the duration of cold symptoms by 1–2 days.

**Q: When should I switch from home care to seeing a doctor?**
A: Seek professional medical advice if you experience persistent high fever, difficulty breathing, severe sinus pain, symptoms that worsen after initial improvement, or if you have a weakened immune system or chronic health conditions.
