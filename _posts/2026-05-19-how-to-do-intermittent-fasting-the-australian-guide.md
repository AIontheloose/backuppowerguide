## 1. What Is Intermittent Fasting?

Intermittent fasting is a pattern of eating that alternates between periods of eating and fasting. Unlike traditional diets that focus on *what* you eat, IF focuses on *when* you eat. The most common protocols include:

- **16/8** – 16 h fast, 8 h eating window  
- **12/12** – 12 h fast, 12 h eating window  
- **5:2** – 5 days normal eating, 2 days 500–600 kcal  
- **Eat‑Stop‑Eat** – 24 h fast once or twice a week  

In 2026, the 12/12 protocol is the most accessible for Australians due to its flexibility around work hours, school schedules, and the often hot climate that encourages lighter meals.

## 2. Choosing Your Fasting Schedule

### Factors to Consider

| Factor | Why It Matters | How to Apply in Australia |
|--------|----------------|---------------------------|
| **Lifestyle** | Work hours, family commitments | Align fasting window with school drop‑offs or after‑work dinners |
| **Climate** | Hot summers can increase thirst | Opt for a later start to avoid dehydration during peak heat |
| **Health Goals** | Weight loss, metabolic health, longevity | 16/8 for quick results; 12/12 for maintenance |
| **Experience Level** | Beginner vs. seasoned fasters | Start with 12/12 before progressing to 16/8 |

### Practical Starter Plan

1. **Week 1–2** – 12/12 (fast from 7 p.m. to 7 a.m.)  
2. **Week 3–4** – 14/10 (fast from 8 p.m. to 10 a.m.)  
3. **Week 5–6** – 16/8 (fast from 9 p.m. to 1 p.m.)  

Monitor how you feel. If you experience dizziness or excessive hunger, consider reverting to a shorter fast.

## Pro Tips for Success

1. **Start with a “Fasting Buddy”** – Pair up with a friend or family member to share progress and keep each other accountable.  
2. **Use the “Fasting Calendar” feature on FastTrack** – Mark your fasting days, set reminders for hydration, and log meals.  
3. **Pre‑pack Your Meals** – Spend 30 min on Sunday preparing salads, grilled veggies, and protein portions for the week.  
4. **Adjust to the Southern Hemisphere** – In winter, keep a warm drink during the early part of your fast to stave off chills.  
5. **Listen to Your Body** – If you feel faint or dizzy, break the fast with a small protein‑rich snack and reassess the fasting length.  
6. **Incorporate Light Exercise** – A brisk 20‑minute walk after breaking your fast can boost insulin sensitivity.  
7. **Track Electrolytes** – Use a simple spreadsheet to log sodium, potassium, and magnesium intake; this helps avoid cramps.  
8. **Celebrate Milestones** – Treat yourself (non‑food reward) after 30 days of consistent fasting; it reinforces positive behaviour.

## FAQ

**Q1: Can I do intermittent fasting if I’m pregnant or breastfeeding?**  
*A1:* It’s best to consult your GP before starting IF during pregnancy or lactation. Some practitioners recommend a 12/12 or 14/10 schedule with careful nutrient planning.

**Q2: Will I lose muscle mass if I fast?**  
*A2:* As long as you consume adequate protein (≥1.2 g/kg body weight) and engage in resistance training, muscle loss is minimal. The meal‑replacement shakes can help meet protein targets.

**Q3: How do I handle a busy workday with meetings and deadlines?**  
*A3:* Plan your eating window around lunch and dinner. Keep a portable shaker bottle with protein powder or a low‑calorie snack (e.g., a handful of almonds) for quick energy.

**Q4: Is intermittent fasting safe for people with diabetes?**  
*A4:* IF can improve glycaemic control, but you must monitor blood sugar closely and adjust medication as directed by your endocrinologist. Start with a 12/12 window and gradually increase.
