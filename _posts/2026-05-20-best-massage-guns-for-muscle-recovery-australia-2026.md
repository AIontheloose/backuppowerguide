### What to Look for in a Massage Gun

Navigating the 2026 market can feel overwhelming, but three specs consistently predict real-world performance. I recommend focusing on these metrics to ensure you get a device that actually works for your body and lifestyle.

1.  **Stall Force:** This is the motor's muscle. A higher stall force means the gun won't bog down when you press hard against a tight knot. Aim for at least 15kg of stall force for deep tissue work; lower forces may feel ineffective on dense muscle groups.
2.  **Amplitude:** This refers to how far the head travels back and forth. For reaching deep muscles like the glutes or lats without bouncing off the surface, look for an amplitude between 10mm and 16mm.
3.  **Battery Life:** In 2026, USB-C fast charging is standard. Prioritise models offering 3 to 6 hours of runtime. You don't want your recovery session cut short, especially if you are travelling for training camps or business trips.

I also recommend prioritising models with multiple attachment heads and adjustable speed settings. ### FAQ: Massage Gun Buying Guide

**How often should I use a massage gun?**
For most users, 1 to 2 sessions per week is sufficient for maintenance, while athletes might benefit from daily use. Limit each muscle group to 2–3 minutes to avoid tissue irritation.

**What's the difference between amplitude and stall force?**
Amplitude refers to the depth of the percussion stroke (how deep it hits), while stall force measures how much resistance the gun can handle before stopping. Higher stall force ensures consistent performance on tight muscles.

**Are massage guns safe for beginners?**
Yes, provided you follow basic guidelines. Avoid using the gun directly on bones, joints, or sensitive areas like the neck and head. Start with the lowest speed and let the device do the work.

**Do I need a specific attachment for sore muscles?**
The round or flat head is best for large muscle groups like quads and back. For trigger points, a bullet or fork attachment can target specific knots more effectively.
