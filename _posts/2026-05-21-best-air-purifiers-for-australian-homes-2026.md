### Why Air Purifiers Matter in 2026

Australia’s **Australian climate** presents a unique set of challenges for indoor air quality. Dry, dusty summer months often bring fine particulate matter, pollen, and windblown sand, while the wetter seasons encourage mould spores and elevated humidity. Indoor activities like cooking, pet grooming, and even furniture off-gassing further compound the issue. In 2026, the focus has shifted from luxury to necessity: maintaining optimal **indoor air quality** is a practical step toward reducing allergen exposure and supporting respiratory comfort.

When shopping, prioritise units that comply with **AS 4005.1-2017** (general requirements for air-purifying devices) and **AS 4005.2-2017** (performance testing standards). While marketing often uses the term “HEPA‑certified”, Australian consumer units typically list “True HEPA” or provide capture efficiency in a technical datasheet. A genuine True HEPA filter will capture 99.97% of particles at 0.3 µm, which effectively traps pollen, dust mite debris, and pet dander. Always verify manufacturer datasheets rather than relying solely on packaging claims.

### Maintenance & Sustainability

Proper maintenance ensures your investment performs as intended. Most units include a washable pre-filter that should be vacuumed or rinsed monthly to extend the lifespan of the main HEPA and carbon layers. Avoid using abrasive cleaners or harsh chemicals on filter media. When it’s time to dispose of old filters, check whether the manufacturer offers recycling programs, as some HEPA materials can be processed through specialised waste streams. Keeping a simple maintenance log helps you stay on schedule and prevents unexpected drops in filtration performance.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How often should I replace the filter in my air purifier?**
Most True HEPA and carbon filters should be replaced every six to twelve months, depending on your local air quality, household size, and usage frequency. Many modern units include digital filter-life indicators that calculate replacement schedules based on actual runtime rather than calendar dates. Ignoring timely **filter replacement** can lead to reduced airflow, increased noise, and diminished allergen capture, so always follow the manufacturer’s guidelines and check for authentic replacement parts.

**2. Will an air purifier eliminate mould and mildew from my home?**
An air purifier can capture airborne mould spores and reduce humidity-related allergens, but it cannot resolve the underlying moisture source that causes mould growth. You’ll need to address leaks, improve cross-ventilation, and use a dehumidifier in persistently damp areas. If mould is visible on walls or ceilings, consult a licensed building inspector or environmental health professional to ensure safe remediation and prevent further indoor air quality degradation.

**3. Is it safe to run a smart air purifier in a bedroom overnight?**
Yes, provided you select a unit with a dedicated sleep mode that operates below 30 dB and disables bright indicator lights. Many **smart air purifier** models now feature automatic light dimming and whisper-quiet fan profiles specifically designed for nighttime use. Ensure the device is placed at least one metre away from your sleeping area to maintain comfortable airflow without direct drafts. Always verify that the unit complies with Australian electrical safety standards before continuous overnight operation.

**4. How do I calculate the right CADR rating for my space?**
Multiply your room’s length by its width to get the square footage, then select a unit with a CADR rating that covers roughly 75% of that area. For example, a 20 m² room would benefit from a CADR of approximately 150–200 cfm for effective cleaning. If you live in a high-pollen or dusty region, consider upgrading to a higher CADR model to compensate for frequent window opening or outdoor particulate infiltration.
