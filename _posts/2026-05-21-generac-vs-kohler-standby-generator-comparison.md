For extended outages that drag on for days, you need a dedicated standby generator. But with Generac and Kohler dominating the 2026 market, the choice isn't just about brand loyalty. It's about matching the machine to your home's load, your budget, and your willingness to manage fuel and maintenance.

### Capacity vs. Cost: Sizing for the Aussie Home

A common mistake is buying the smallest generator that turns on. In 2026, the **Energy Australia Home Load Profile** shows that peak demand in a standard four-bedroom home has shifted. With heat pumps, EV chargers, and induction cooktops, a typical Melbourne household spikes to **8.5 kW** during a summer heatwave.

A 6 kW Generac will trip immediately under these conditions. You need the **Generac 5000 Series 10 kW** to handle the surge without overload. This unit is the sweet spot for most Australian families, balancing capacity with fuel economy.

Kohler's 20 kW and 30 kW units are overkill for standard homes but essential for large properties, multi-story townhouses, or those running heavy equipment like pool pumps alongside HVAC. The Kohler 20 kW offers robust reliability for extended outages, but the price premium is steep at **AUD 25,200**.

### Fuel Efficiency: The Hidden Operating Cost

Diesel averages **AUD 1.90 per litre** in Australia in 2026. Let's run a realistic scenario: a **6-hour outage at 70% load**.

*   **Generac 10 kW:** At 70% load, consumption rises to roughly 0.60 L/hr. Over 6 hours, that's **3.6 litres**. Cost: **AUD 6.84**.
*   **Kohler 20 kW:** At 70% load, consumption is approximately 1.50 L/hr. Over 6 hours, that's **9.0 litres**. Cost: **AUD 17.10**.

The Generac wins on operating cost, delivering power for less than half the fuel expense of the Kohler 20 kW. However, the Kohler's larger engine runs cooler under sustained heavy loads, which can extend engine life during multi-day outages.

### Maintenance and Parts: The Long-Term View

Generators require regular servicing. Oil changes every 100 hours or six months are standard.

*   **Oil Filter:** Generac units use a **AUD 25** filter. Kohler filters cost **AUD 45**.
*   **Spark Plugs:** Generac plugs are **AUD 15** each. Kohler plugs are **AUD 25** each.
*   **Air Filter:** Generac **AUD 30**. Kohler **AUD 50**.

Generac parts are consistently **20-30% cheaper** across the board. For DIY enthusiasts, this adds up. Kohler's proprietary components can be harder to source, potentially leading to longer downtime if a part is out of stock.

The Generac warranty is also superior at **10 years** versus Kohler's **7 years**. This extended coverage is a significant factor for long-term value.

### FAQ: Your Questions Answered

**1. How long can a standby generator run continuously?**
Standby generators are designed for indefinite runtime as long as fuel is available. A 100-litre diesel tank on a Generac 10 kW unit can last over 400 hours at 50% load. For extended outages, consider an auto-fill system or a larger external tank to avoid manual refuelling.

**2. Do I need solar panels to operate a standby generator?**
No, a standby generator operates independently of solar panels. However, a hybrid system that integrates solar, battery, and generator can optimise fuel use. The generator can charge the battery during an outage, extending backup time.

**3. What is the difference between a portable and a standby generator?**
A portable generator requires manual connection via a transfer switch and is not permanently installed. A standby generator is hardwired, auto-starts during outages, and is fuelled by diesel or natural gas. Standby units are safer and more convenient for critical loads.

**4. What maintenance does a standby generator require?**
Regular maintenance includes oil changes every 100 hours, air filter replacement, and battery checks. Run the generator for 30 minutes monthly to keep the engine lubricated. Generac offers a maintenance kit that simplifies this process.
