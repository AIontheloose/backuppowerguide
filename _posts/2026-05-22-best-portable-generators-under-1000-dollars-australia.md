### Frequently Asked Questions

**What size generator do I actually need for home backup?**  
Start with your critical loads: lighting, fridge, sump pump, and comms equipment typically draw 1.5–2.5 kW. If you’re running an air conditioner or electric hot water system, bump to 3.5–5 kW. Always add a 20–30% surge margin for motor startup currents.

**Can I run a generator indoors or in a garage?**  
Absolutely not. Carbon monoxide is odourless and lethal. Always position units at least 3 metres from windows, doors, or vents, with exhaust directed away from occupied spaces. A mains-powered CO alarm inside your home is non-negotiable.

**How often should I service a portable generator?**  
Follow the manufacturer’s interval, but as a rule: change the oil every 50 hours or annually, replace the air filter annually (or more frequently in dusty conditions), and run the unit under load monthly to prevent fuel degradation and carburetor varnish buildup.

**Is inverter technology worth the premium?**  
If you’re powering sensitive electronics (laptops, medical devices, audio gear), yes. Inverter generators deliver clean, stable power with lower THD (<3%) and significantly quieter operation. For heavy tools or resistive loads, a conventional 3000W unit remains more cost-effective.

**What’s the difference between 3000W and 3500W units in Australia?**  
The extra 500W covers startup surges for larger appliances and improves efficiency under partial load. In hot climates, 3500W units often run cooler and quieter at equivalent outputs, making them the pragmatic choice for most Australian households.
