## FAQ: DIY Patio Paving in 2026

**Q: Do I need council approval for a DIY patio?**  
A: It depends on your local council and the patio’s footprint. Generally, paved areas under 30m² that don’t alter natural drainage lines or encroach on boundary setbacks don’t require development approval. Always verify with your local council or a private building certifier before breaking ground.

**Q: Can I lay pavers directly over an existing concrete slab?**  
A: It’s possible but rarely ideal. Adding height can create trip hazards, block door thresholds, and trap moisture. If the slab is structurally sound and level, adjustable paver pedestals offer a safer alternative. Otherwise, strip and rebuild a proper 100mm road base for long-term stability.

**Q: How do I stop polymeric sand from washing out in heavy rain?**  
A: Allow 24–48 hours of dry curing before exposing joints to water, and avoid high-pressure washing. For regions with intense downpours, apply a breathable joint sealer or opt for a permeable gravel or resin-bound edge to manage runoff without compromising joint integrity.

**Q: What’s the most common DIY mistake?**  
A: Rushing the base preparation. A compacted, laser-leveled sub-base and proper edge restraint are non-negotiable. Skip them, and you’ll be releveling pavers within 12–18 months. Take the time to do it right the first time.
