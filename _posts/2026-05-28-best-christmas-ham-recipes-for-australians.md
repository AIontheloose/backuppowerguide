### Choosing Your Ham: Size, Source, and Smart Savings

The first decision is always size. With Christmas landing in the heart of summer, families are increasingly opting for smarter portions. While the 10kg ham at $250 sounds like a bargain for a large gathering, what I’ve found in 2026 is that a 4kg ham for roughly $120 is the true sweet spot for most modern households. It’s manageable, leaves ample room for lighter summer sides, and significantly reduces food waste. From an environmental standpoint, choosing a 4kg ham over a 10kg counterpart can cut unnecessary carbon emissions tied to over-purchasing, prolonged refrigeration, and eventual landfill waste. If you do have a large crew, consider splitting the cost with neighbours and hosting a collaborative Christmas lunch instead.

When it comes to sourcing, quality is everything. Australian-grown hams, particularly those from Queensland labelled as “Crown” or “Murray”, often boast a superior texture and flavour profile that holds up beautifully to glazes. As we move through 2026, keep an eye on the Australian Food Standards Code (Section 3.5). Always look for “Australian-made” or “Australian-grown” labels to ensure you’re supporting local producers and meeting rigorous regulatory standards for food safety and traceability.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I glaze the ham earlier in the cooking process?**
A: It's best to apply your glaze during the final 30–45 minutes of roasting. If you add sugary glazes too early, the sugars will burn and turn bitter, leaving a charred taste rather than a sticky, caramelized finish.

**Q: Is a bone-in ham better than boneless?**
A: For most home cooks, bone-in is superior. The bone acts as an insulator, helping the meat cook more evenly, and it contributes significantly to moisture and flavor. You'll also find the meat stays juicier right up to the bone.

**Q: How do I carve the ham properly?**
A: Always use a long, sharp carving knife and slice against the grain. Wait until after the ham has rested to carve; cutting into hot meat will cause the juices to run out, leaving the slices dry.

**Q: How long do leftovers last?**
A: Properly stored in an airtight container, leftover ham will keep in the refrigerator for 3 to 4 days. For longer storage, you can freeze slices for up to two months.
