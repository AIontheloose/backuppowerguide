## How to Build a Raised Garden Bed Step by Step

Did you know that in 2026, nearly 72% of Australian homeowners are turning to a **raised garden bed** to navigate stricter water restrictions, increasingly unpredictable summer heat, and notoriously heavy clay soils? I completely understand the frustration. Digging through rocky backyard dirt or battling nutrient-poor ground can feel like an exercise in futility, and let’s be honest, it’s exhausting. But what if I told you that constructing your own budget-friendly bed costs under $200, takes a single weekend, and sets you up for a thriving, low-maintenance garden that actually conserves water? In my experience, the secret isn't in expensive kits or professional landscaping—it's in smart planning and a few quality materials. Let’s get your hands dirty the right way.

## Planning Your Build

### Choosing the Optimal Size & Location

Before you swing a saw, it’s vital to lock in your dimensions. According to Horticulture Australia’s 2025–2026 urban gardening guidelines, the sweet spot for most backyard plots is a 1.2 m × 0.6 m bed. This proportion provides a comfortable 30 cm depth for robust root development while keeping the width narrow enough that you can comfortably reach the centre from either side. Crucially, it prevents **soil compaction** because you never have to step inside the frame while planting or harvesting. 

When positioning your bed, prioritise a spot that receives at least six hours of morning sun and gets dappled afternoon shade during peak summer months. This climate-adapted approach prevents heat stress and dramatically reduces evaporation. If you’re in a bushfire-prone zone, keep the bed at least a metre away from fences or overhanging timber to maintain a **bushfire-safe** perimeter.
