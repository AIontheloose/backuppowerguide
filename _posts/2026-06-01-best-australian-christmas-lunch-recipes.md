## Best Australian Christmas Lunch Recipes for 2026

There is something truly magical about a December lunchdown where the sun is shining, the cicadas are humming, and the garden table groans under the weight of good food and even better company. In 2026, feeding a crowd doesn’t have to drain your savings. With smart shopping, a dash of creativity, and a few clever kitchen tricks, you can craft a truly festive Australian Christmas menu that’s as budget-friendly as it is beautiful. Whether you’re hosting a coastal gathering or a backyard bash, I’ve put together a collection of tested, family-approved recipes that celebrate summer, support local producers, and keep the kitchen stress-free. Let’s get cooking!

### 1. The Heart of the Meal: Turkey & Ham

The classic **turkey and ham Christmas lunch** remains the undisputed star of our summer tables. This year, you can snag a 5 kg free-range turkey for around **$75 AUD** and a 200 g pack of quality smoked ham for roughly **$16 AUD**. Here’s how to get them perfectly cooked without the guesswork.

#### Brined & Roasted Free-Range Turkey
- **Ingredients:** 5 kg whole turkey, 200 g sea salt, 150 g brown sugar, 2 L cold water, zest of 2 lemons, 1 tbsp lemon myrtle, 1 bunch thyme, 1 onion, halved.
- **Prep time:** 20 mins (+ 4 hours brining) | **Cook time:** 2 hours 15 mins | **Yield:** Serves 8–10
- **Method:** Dissolve salt and sugar in 1 L of hot water, then cool with the remaining cold water and lemon myrtle. Submerge the turkey in the brine for 4 hours. Remove, pat dry, and rub with thyme, lemon zest, and olive oil. Roast at 180 °C for 15 minutes per 500 g, basting every 30 minutes, until the thickest part of the thigh hits 75 °C. Rest for 20 minutes before carving.

#### Honey-Pineapple Glazed Smoked Ham
For a sweet-savory companion, warm your pre-cooked ham with a glaze of 200 mL pineapple juice, 3 tbsp runny honey, and a pinch of cracked black pepper. Simmer until slightly syrupy, then brush over the ham during the final 10 minutes of roasting. ### 2. Sweet & Savoury Sides

#### Roasted Sweet Potatoes & Quick Cranberry Sauce
- **Ingredients:** 1 kg organic sweet potatoes, 2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tsp smoked paprika, sea salt; 250 g fresh or frozen cranberries, 100 g caster sugar, juice of 1 orange.
- **Prep time:** 15 mins | **Cook time:** 35 mins | **Yield:** Serves 8
- **Method:** Toss chopped sweet potatoes with oil, paprika, and salt. Roast at 200 °C on a lined tray for 30 minutes, turning halfway. While they cook, simmer cranberries, sugar, and orange juice in a saucepan for 8 minutes until the berries burst and the sauce thickens. Set aside to cool slightly.

#### Baked Beans & Lemon Myrtle Butter
- **Ingredients:** 2 x 400 g cans of premium baked beans, 2 tbsp tomato paste, 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 1 tsp mustard powder; 150 g unsalted butter, 2 tbsp finely chopped lemon myrtle, pinch of flaky salt.
- **Prep time:** 10 mins | **Cook time:** 15 mins | **Yield:** Serves 8
- **Method:** Drain beans, mix with tomato paste, Worcestershire, and mustard. Warm in a casserole dish at 180 °C for 15 minutes. Blend softened butter with lemon myrtle and salt until fragrant. Serve alongside warm sourdough rolls.

### 3. Light & Fresh: Salads & Seafood

Summer Christmas ideas thrive on brightness. These **coastal Christmas lunch ideas** keep the palate refreshed between richer dishes.

#### Citrus & Avocado Salad with Wattleseed Crumble
- **Ingredients:** 3 ripe avocados, 2 navel oranges, 1 red onion, 2 cups mixed greens, 2 tbsp raw wattleseed, 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, juice of 1 lime, 1 tsp honey.
- **Prep time:** 15 mins | **Yield:** Serves 8
- **Method:** Segment oranges over a bowl to catch the juice. Dice avocados and thinly slice onion. Whisk olive oil, lime juice, and honey into the orange juice for the dressing. Toss greens with dressing, arrange avocados and orange segments on top, and crumble toasted wattleseed over the salad for a subtle, nutty finish.

#### Grilled Barramundi with Coconut-Lime Drizzle
- **Ingredients:** 4 x 150 g barramundi fillets, 200 mL coconut milk, zest of 2 limes, 1 clove garlic (minced), 1 tbsp fish sauce, 1 tsp brown sugar.
- **Prep time:** 10 mins | **Cook time:** 8 mins | **Yield:** Serves 4
- **Method:** Whisk coconut milk, lime zest, garlic, fish sauce, and sugar. Brush fillets with the mixture. Grill on medium-high heat for 3–4 minutes per side until opaque and flaky. Serve with steamed baby bok choy or native pepperberry.

### 4. Starters & Festive Desserts

No **Australian Christmas menu** is complete without a vibrant starter and a show-stopping **festive Australian dessert**.

#### Mango & Avocado Salsa Starter
- **Ingredients:** 2 ripe mangoes, 2 avocados, 1 red chilli, 1/2 cup fresh coriander, juice of 2 limes, 1 tsp sesame oil.
- **Prep time:** 15 mins | **Yield:** Serves 8 as a starter
- **Method:** Dice mango and avocado into uniform cubes. Gently fold in finely sliced chilli, chopped coriander, lime juice, and sesame oil. Serve in hollowed-out cherry tomatoes or with crisp rice crackers for a light, refreshing opening to your feast.

#### Chocolate-Fruit Pavlova with Native Twists
- **Ingredients:** 1 kg pavlova mix, 400 mL full-fat cream, 2 tbsp cocoa powder, 1 tsp vanilla bean paste, 200 g mixed berries, 2 tbsp finger lime segments, 1 tbsp ground wattleseed.
- **Prep time:** 20 mins | **Cook time:** 1 hour 15 mins | **Yield:** Serves 10
- **Method:** Whip cream with cocoa and vanilla until soft peaks form. Spread over a cooled pavlova shell. Top with berries, finger lime, and a dusting of wattleseed. The tart native citrus cuts beautifully through the sweet meringue and cocoa.

### 8. Frequently Asked Questions

**1. How do I keep my turkey moist during the roast?**  
Brining the turkey for several hours before cooking helps the meat retain moisture throughout the roasting process. Additionally, basting the bird every 30 minutes with pan juices or a light olive oil rub prevents the skin from drying out. Using a reliable thermometer ensures you pull it from the oven the moment it reaches 75 °C, avoiding overcooking which is the main culprit for dry meat.

**2. What is a good vegetarian alternative for Christmas lunch?**  
A spiced lentil loaf or a roasted pumpkin and quinoa pie makes an excellent centrepiece that rivals traditional meats in both flavour and satisfaction. You can enhance it with native pepperberry, roasted garlic, and a rich mushroom gravy to add depth. Pair it with the same sides—sweet potatoes, cranberry sauce, and lemon myrtle butter—to ensure everyone enjoys a cohesive meal.

**3. Can I prepare the salads ahead of time to reduce stress?**  
Yes, most summer salads can be prepped a few hours in advance, but keep the dressing separate until serving to prevent wilting. Chop and store the avocados in a sealed container with a squeeze of lime to prevent browning. The citrus segments and native berries can also be portioned early, making assembly on the day much quicker and less chaotic.
