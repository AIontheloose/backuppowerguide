## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I leave my Christmas lights outdoors year-round?**  
A: While some commercial-grade lights are built for all-weather use, most residential decorations are designed for seasonal use. Leaving them out past summer invites UV degradation, moisture damage, and shortened lifespans. Pack them away once the decorations come down.

**Q: How do I safely store seasonal decorations?**  
A: Keep everything in airtight, plastic storage bins with silica gel packets to control moisture. Roll light strings instead of wrapping them tightly around your hands to prevent wire fraying. Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.

**Q: Are solar lights reliable during overcast Australian summers?**  
A: Modern solar panels have improved significantly, but prolonged cloud cover can reduce battery charging. For consistently shaded areas, opt for hybrid solar/wired models or stick to low-wattage LED strings with timer functions.

**Q: What’s the best way to protect decorations from bushfire season?**  
A: Never place decorations near dry vegetation or combustible surfaces. Use only UL/CE-certified LED lights with cool-running bulbs, and ensure all power points are weatherproof. When in doubt, keep decorations well away from the house during high-risk periods.

**Q: How can I make my yard festive without attracting wildlife or disturbing neighbours?**  
A: Stick to warm-white LEDs (avoid blue/white which disrupt nocturnal animals), use motion sensors to limit runtime, and keep volume down if you’re adding music. Always check local council guidelines on light pollution and temporary outdoor structures.
