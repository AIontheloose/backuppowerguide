## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Are inflatable pools safe for young children?**  
A: Absolutely, provided you choose a model with a thick, puncture-resistant base and a shallow depth (typically 18–24 inches). Always supervise children around water, and opt for pools with smooth, rounded edges and non-slip interiors.

**Q: How long do modern inflatable pools last in Australia?**  
A: With proper care, premium 2026 models can comfortably last three to five seasons. Avoid dragging them across rough concrete, always use a ground protection tarp, and drain them before heavy rain or winter to prevent mould and material breakdown.

**Q: Do I need an electric pump, or can I inflate them by mouth?**  
A: Smaller models can be mouth-inflated, but an electric or battery-powered pump is highly recommended. It ensures even pressure, saves your lungs, and significantly reduces the risk of seam stress or overinflation.

**Q: Can I leave an inflatable pool in direct Australian sun?**  
A: Look for pools rated with UV-resistant and anti-fade materials. Even then, a lightweight pool cover or shade sail will extend the material’s life and keep the water cooler for longer dips.

**Q: What’s the ideal spot to set up an inflatable pool?**  
A: A flat, level area on grass or soft soil works best. Keep it away from tree roots, sharp objects, and high-traffic zones. Ensure easy access to a garden hose and a clear drainage path for refilling.
