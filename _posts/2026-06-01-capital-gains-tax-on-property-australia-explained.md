### How to Calculate Your CGT Accurately
Calculating your tax liability requires precision. The Australian Taxation Office (ATO) mandates a strict formula that leaves no room for estimation. Follow these steps methodically to avoid overpayment or compliance breaches:

1. **Determine the Gross Capital Gain:** Sale Price minus Purchase Price minus Cost Base. The cost base includes acquisition costs, legal fees, agent commissions, and capital improvements.
   *Example:* Purchase: $500,000 AUD | Sale: $750,000 AUD | Cost Base (fees + renovations): $20,000 AUD. Gross Gain = $230,000 AUD.
2. **Apply the 50% CGT Discount:** Only applies if the asset was held for more than 12 months. Discounted Gain = $230,000 × 50% = $115,000 AUD.
3. **Factor in State-Specific Surcharges:** This step is jurisdiction-dependent. In NSW, a 2% capital gains surcharge

...applies only to non-resident landowners under specific state surcharge regimes, though federal CGT remains uniform across jurisdictions. For most Australian resident individuals, the calculation stops at step two before being added to your marginal tax rate.

**4. **Add to Your Assessable Income:** The discounted gain is taxed as ordinary income at your marginal rate. Using the example above, $115,000 added to a $95,000 salary (assuming a 37% marginal rate + 2% Medicare levy) would result in approximately $46,000 in additional tax payable. The exact liability depends on your total income, deductions, and tax file number status.

**Pro Tips for Compliance & Optimization:**
- **Timing Matters:** Selling before June 30 vs. after July 1 can shift your tax year, potentially keeping you in a lower marginal bracket.
- **Document Everything:** The ATO requires proof of cost base, improvement invoices, and settlement statements. Poor records often result in default assessments.
- **Main Residence Exemption:** If the property was your primary home for the entire ownership period, CGT is generally exempt. Partial exemptions apply if it was rented out.
- **Carry Forward Losses:** Capital losses cannot offset ordinary income but can be carried forward indefinitely to reduce future capital gains.

### **Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: What if I held the asset for less than 12 months?**  
You won’t qualify for the 50% CGT discount. The full gross gain is added to your assessable income and taxed at your marginal rate.

**Q: Do I need to report a CGT event if I made a loss?**  
Yes. All CGT events must be reported in your tax return, even if they result in a loss. Unclaimed losses cannot be carried forward without proper documentation.

**Q: Can I use the CGT discount for shares or cryptocurrency?**  
Yes, provided the asset was held for more than 12 months. The 50% discount applies equally to property, shares, crypto, and other chargeable assets.

**Q: How does negative gearing interact with CGT?**  
Negative gearing offsets rental losses against your taxable income in the holding period, but it does not reduce or eliminate CGT when you eventually sell.

**Q: When should I consult a tax professional?**  
Before signing a contract of sale, after receiving a capital improvement quote, or if your ownership period straddles two financial years. Early planning can save thousands.
