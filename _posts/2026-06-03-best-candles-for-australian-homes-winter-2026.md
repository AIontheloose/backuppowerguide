### Key Factors to Consider

**1. Wax Type**  
Soy wax remains the gold standard for Australian households. It’s plant‑based, burns cleaner, and holds fragrance beautifully. Paraffin is petroleum‑derived, burns slightly faster, and can leave more soot on glass, though it’s still a viable budget option if you’re mindful of ventilation. Beeswax is another excellent, naturally occurring alternative that purifies the air as it burns, though it sits at a higher price point.

**2. Size & Burn Time**  
For everyday use, 200–250 g jars are ideal. If you’re hosting Christmas lunch or a winter dinner party, step up to 500 g or a 1 kg vessel to avoid constant relighting. Smaller tins (100 g) are lovely for gifting or for kids’ rooms where a subtle, low‑intensity scent is preferred.

**3. Wick Management**  
A trimmed wick is non‑negotiable. Keep it at 5 mm before every light. This reduces soot by roughly 30%, prevents mushrooming, and ensures a steady, even melt pool.

**4. Scent Profile**  
Winter scents should feel grounding and homely. Look for blends that layer warm base notes (vanilla, cedar, sandalwood) with brighter top notes (cinnamon, pine, or native eucalyptus). Avoid overly sweet or synthetic‑smelling fragrances, which can quickly become overwhelming in a closed‑up house.

**5. Budget Awareness**  
Quality doesn’t have to mean a premium price tag. By calculating cost‑per‑hour, you’ll quickly see where your money actually goes. I’ve rounded out the 2026 market averages below so you can compare apples with apples.

### Supporting Local & The Eco‑Impact

When it comes to sustainability, wax is only half the story. Soy wax is renewable, biodegradable, and generally produces a lower carbon footprint than petroleum‑based paraffin. However, packaging and sourcing matter just as much. Look for brands that use recycled glass jars, soy‑based inks, and minimal plastic.

Always check for clear ingredient lists, burn instructions, and reputable retail channels to ensure you’re getting a compliant, safe product.

### Safety & Care: Dos and Don’ts

Candles are wonderful, but they demand respect. Follow these steps to keep your home safe and your jars burning cleanly:

**Do:**  
- Light candles on a stable, heat‑resistant surface away from drafts, curtains, and pets.  
- Trim the wick to 5 mm before every lighting to prevent soot and uneven burning.  
- Allow the first burn to reach the full width of the container (usually 1–2 hours) to prevent tunnelling.  
- Use a proper candle snuffer or dip the wick in melted wax to extinguish it safely.  
- Store unused candles in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve scent integrity.

**Don’t:**  
- Never leave a burning candle unattended or sleep with one lit.  
- Don’t burn a candle for more than 4 hours at a time; let the wax cool before relighting.  
- Avoid placing candles near flammable materials, water, or on unstable surfaces.  
- Don’t mix different candle brands or wax types in the same jar; they burn at different rates and can create hazardous flare‑ups.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Do soy candles produce less soot than paraffin?**  
Yes, soy candles generally produce significantly less soot because they burn at a lower temperature and contain no petroleum by‑products. This cleaner burn means fewer black marks on your glass jars and a healthier indoor environment, which is especially important during winter when windows stay closed. Always ensure proper ventilation and trim your wick to maintain that clean, steady flame.

**2. Are wooden wick candles better for ambient noise?**  
Wooden wicks do create a gentle crackling sound that many people find soothing, but they burn slightly faster and require more frequent trimming than cotton wicks. If you’re after a quiet, consistent burn for reading or sleeping, a cotton wick with a soy base is usually the more reliable choice. Both can be wonderful, but it really depends on whether you prioritise sound or longevity.

**3. How should I dispose of a used candle jar?**  
Once the wax is fully consumed, rinse the jar with warm water and remove any adhesive labels. Soy wax is biodegradable, so you can compost the residual wax or wipe it out with a paper towel. The glass jar is fully recyclable through your local council’s glass programme, and many local brands now encourage refilling or returning jars for a discount on future purchases.

**4. Is it safe to burn a candle in the bedroom for sleep?**  
It’s generally safe if you follow strict precautions: burn for no more than 2 hours, place it at least 1 metre from the bed, and ensure the room is well‑ventilated. Some people find the scent of vanilla or soft cedar calming, while others are sensitive to fragrance during sleep. If you’re unsure, opt for a phthalate‑free, lightly scented option or simply enjoy the warm glow without the fragrance for a restful night.
