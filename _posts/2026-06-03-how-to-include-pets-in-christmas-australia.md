### Outdoor Safety for Summer Celebrations

Many Aussie families host backyard BBQs or head to the coast for Christmas Day. While the sunshine is glorious, our pets need extra vigilance during outdoor festivities. Heatstroke is a very real threat, so always ensure your furry friend has access to a shaded, well-ventilated retreat away from the main crowd. Bring a portable water bowl and encourage frequent drinking breaks, even if they don’t seem thirsty. If you’re heading to the beach, keep your dog on a secure leash near the dunes where stray traffic and unexpected waves can startle them. Never leave pets unattended in parked cars, even with the windows cracked, as temperatures can skyrocket in minutes. A simple spray bottle of cool water and a damp towel draped over their back can work wonders for heatstroke prevention when the mercury climbs.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Can my pet eat Christmas food?**
No, you should never let your pet eat human Christmas food. Many festive dishes contain ingredients like chocolate, onions, garlic, and excessive salt or fat, which are highly toxic to dogs and cats. Even cooked bones can splinter and cause internal damage. Instead, offer your pet a small portion of plain, unseasoned meats or pet-safe vegetables to ensure they stay safe and healthy during the celebrations.

**2. How do I stop my dog from drinking the tree water?**
Tree water can be dangerous because it often contains fertilizers, pesticides, or preservatives that are harmful if ingested. To prevent this, secure the base of your tree with a heavy guard or place the tree in a corner where your dog cannot reach it. You can also add a bitter apple spray to the water to deter drinking. Ensuring your tree is firmly anchored and out of reach is the most effective way to keep your furry friend away from the stand.

**3. What should I do if my pet ingests a toy or ornament?**
If your pet swallows a toy or ornament, monitor them closely for signs of distress such as vomiting, lethargy, or loss of appetite. If you suspect a blockage, contact your veterinarian immediately. Do not wait for symptoms to worsen, as early intervention is crucial. Keep the packaging or a sample of the item handy to show your vet, as this helps them determine the best course of action for your pet’s safety.

**4. Are Christmas sweaters necessary for Aussie summers?**
Sweaters are generally not necessary and can be dangerous for pets during an Australian summer Christmas. Since we celebrate in the height of summer, most dogs and cats do not need extra warmth and are at risk of overheating. If you do dress your pet for a photo, use a lightweight, breathable cotton garment for a very short duration in a cool, air-conditioned room. Always prioritise your pet’s comfort and well-being over festive fashion.
