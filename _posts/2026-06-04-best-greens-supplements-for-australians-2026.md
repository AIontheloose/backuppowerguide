### Practical Integration Guide

Dosing is straightforward: one scoop (typically 7–10g) mixed with 250–300ml of water, almond milk, or blended into a morning smoothie. I recommend taking it on an empty stomach or with a light breakfast to maximise absorption. Pair your greens with a source of healthy fat (like half an avocado or a tablespoon of chia seeds) to enhance the uptake of fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, and K. 

Many of my clients notice improved digestion within two weeks and steadier energy levels by week four. However, results vary based on diet, sleep, and activity levels. If you experience mild bloating initially, start with half a scoop and gradually increase over seven days. Always store your powder in a cool, dry place and seal tightly after each use to preserve potency.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I take greens supplements if I'm on prescription medication?**  
Yes, but you should absolutely consult your GP or pharmacist first. Some greens contain vitamin K, which can interact with blood thinners like warfarin, while others may affect how your liver processes certain drugs. A healthcare professional can review your medication list and advise on safe timing or dosage adjustments to avoid unwanted interactions.

**Are greens powders suitable for vegans and keto dieters?**  
Most high-quality greens powders are naturally vegan and free from animal-derived ingredients. For keto dieters, check the carbohydrate count carefully; some blends include fruit powders or sweeteners that can push you out of ketosis. Opt for unsweetened versions with minimal carbs and pair them with keto-friendly fats to maintain your metabolic state.

**How do I know if a greens supplement is actually tested for quality?**  
Look for independent third-party testing certificates, such as NSF Certified for Sport or Informed Choice, which verify purity, heavy metal screening, and label accuracy. Reputable brands will publish these results on their websites or upon request. Avoid products that rely solely on manufacturer claims without verifiable lab data.

**Should I take greens in the morning or evening?**  
Most people prefer morning dosing to support daytime energy and digestion, but the timing matters less than consistency. If you're sensitive to caffeine (some greens contain matcha or green tea extract), take it before 2 PM. For evening routines, choose a caffeine-free blend and pair it with a light snack to prevent sleep disruption.
