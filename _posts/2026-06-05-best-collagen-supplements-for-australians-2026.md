### Why the Hype Around Collagen Has Intensified in 2026

The conversation around collagen has shifted from superficial beauty benefits to comprehensive systemic health. We now understand that collagen is not just a structural protein for skin; it is the primary component of our connective tissues, tendons, ligaments, and the gut lining. As we age, our body's natural production declines at a rate of approximately 1–2% per year. By the time many of us reach our late 30s or 40s, we are effectively losing a pound of collagen every decade, which manifests as joint discomfort, reduced skin elasticity, and slower recovery times.

The science behind collagen has deepened significantly in recent years. A pivotal 2025 meta-analysis published in the *Journal of Nutrition* highlighted that hydrolysed collagen peptides not only support dermal structure but also contribute to the integrity of the gut lining. The amino acids glycine and glutamine found in collagen are vital for maintaining the mucosal barrier, which is increasingly relevant as we focus on holistic gut health. This dual-action benefit—supporting both physical mobility and digestive resilience—explains why collagen has moved into the top tier of supplements for active Australians.

Furthermore, the regulatory landscape has tightened. The TGA's 2025 updates mean you can trust the ARTG listing more than ever. Manufacturers can no longer make vague 'anti-ageing' claims without robust evidence. This protects you from misleading marketing and ensures that products on the shelf have been assessed for safety and manufacturing quality. When you see a product on the ARTG, it confirms it meets Australian safety and manufacturing standards.

### How to Read a Collagen Label: What Actually Matters

Navigating supplement aisles requires a critical eye. Not all collagen is created equal, and understanding the terminology is crucial for making an informed purchase.

**1. Hydrolysed Collagen vs. Gelatin**
You will often see the term "hydrolysed collagen" or "collagen peptides" on labels. This is essential. Hydro

lysed collagen has been broken down into smaller peptide chains. This process significantly enhances bioavailability, meaning your body can absorb and utilize the amino acids more efficiently than it could with gelatin, which is unhydrolysed and solidifies in cool temperatures. For supplements, hydrolysed collagen is the gold standard.

**2. Source and Collagen Types**
Not all collagen comes from the same source. Bovine collagen typically offers Types I and III, which are vital for skin, hair, nails, and gut health. Marine collagen is rich in Type I and is often preferred for skin elasticity and absorption speed. If you're targeting joint health, chicken-derived collagen may provide Type II, which supports cartilage. Always check the source to align with your specific goals.

**3. Dosage and Proprietary Blends**
Look for transparency. Effective clinical studies often use doses between 2.5g and 10g daily. Avoid products that list "proprietary blends" without specifying the exact amount of collagen per serving. You need to know exactly what you're consuming to gauge efficacy.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does it take to see results from collagen supplementation?**
A: Consistency is key. Most clinical studies suggest that noticeable improvements in skin hydration, elasticity, and joint comfort typically appear after 8 to 12 weeks of daily supplementation.

**Q: Is the TGA's 2025 update relevant to my current collagen supply?**
A: If your product is listed on the ARTG, it meets the current standards. The updates tighten future marketing claims and evidence requirements, but existing listings remain valid provided they meet the ongoing safety criteria. Always check the ARTG for the most current status.

**Q: Can I take collagen with other supplements?**
A: Collagen pairs well with Vitamin C, which aids in the body's natural collagen synthesis. However, always check with your healthcare provider before combining supplements, especially if you have underlying health conditions.

**Q: How do I verify a product's safety?**
A: Visit the TGA's ARTG database and search the product name or sponsor. A valid entry confirms the product has been assessed for safety, quality, and therapeutic goods compliance.
