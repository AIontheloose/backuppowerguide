Don't be a hero; be prepared. Aussie roads can be unpredictable. Before you turn the key, run through this checklist:
*   **Seatbelts & Airbags:** Ensure all occupants are buckled. Check that your airbag warning light stays off.
*   **Spare Tyre & Jack:** A puncture in the Blue Mountains isn't a joke. Make sure your spare is inflated and you have the tools to change it.
*   **First-Aid Kit:** Keep a fully stocked kit accessible. You can grab a reliable one via this [Car First Aid Kit Australia](https://www.amazon.com.au/s?k=Car+First+Aid+Kit+Australia&tag=owlno-22).

### Route 1: The Blue Mountains – The Weekend Warrior

**Distance:** ~100 km one way  
**Drive Time:** ~1.5 to 2 hours  
**Road Type:** Sealed, winding mountain roads

The Blue Mountains are the classic Sydney escape. While some might argue a "3-hour drive" is standard, that's usually due to weekend traffic bottlenecks at the M4 interchange or heavy tourist loads. Under normal conditions, it's a brisk 1.5-hour drive. However, on a Saturday morning, add an extra hour to your ETA.

**The Itinerary:**
Start in **Katoomba**. The Three Sisters are iconic, but the real magic is in the lesser-known spots. Drive up to **Govetts Leap** for a stunning cliff-top view without the crowds. Then, tackle **O'Connell's Gap**. This is a technical section with blind corners; drive it slowly.

**Cost Breakdown:**
*   **Fuel:** ~10 litres round trip. At $1.55/L, that's roughly **$15.50**.
*   **Accommodation:** A budget stay at a Katoomba hotel runs about **$120 per night**. For a cheaper option, the **Blue Mountains Camping** site charges around **$35 per night** for a basic site. ### FAQ: Blue Mountains Road Trip & Camping

**Q: When is the best time to visit the Blue Mountains?**
**A:** Spring (September to November) and Autumn (March to May) are ideal. The weather is mild, visibility is clear, and the waterfalls are flowing. Avoid mid-summer; the heat puts unnecessary strain on your engine coolant, and bushfire risks can close access roads without warning.

**Q: Is there mobile signal in the Blue Mountains?**
**A:** Signal is spotty to non-existent in many valleys and remote free camping zones. This is exactly why Dan insists on downloading offline maps before you leave. Assume you'll be off-grid and plan your route with that in mind.

**Q: What wildlife should I watch out for?**
**A:** Kangaroos and wallabies are common, especially at dawn and dusk. Drive with high beams at night where legal, and slow down well before the tree line. Never stop to feed wildlife; it's dangerous for you, the animals, and your vehicle.

**Q: Can I camp for free in the Blue Mountains National Park?**
**A:** Yes, but regulations change frequently. Some areas require a permit, while others are free but strictly "leave no trace." Always check the latest NSW National Parks rules and local council bylaws before you arrive to avoid fines.

**Q: Are there fuel stations deep in the ranges?**
**A:** Katoomba and Leura have fuel, but once you head into the bush or toward smaller towns like Lawson or Megalong, stations are scarce. Fill up in Katoomba before venturing out, and keep your tank above half when exploring remote tracks.
