### **Frequently Asked Questions**

**Q: How do I know my donation actually reaches the cause?**  
A: Always choose registered charities with transparent financial reporting. In Australia, look for ABN registration, annual reports on their website, and endorsements from independent watchdogs like Charity Navigator or GiveWell. Reputable organizations clearly outline how funds are allocated between administration, programs, and outreach.

**Q: Can I still give a physical gift alongside a charity donation?**  
A: Absolutely. Many givers pair a small, meaningful token—a handwritten note, a locally crafted candle, or a fair-trade treat—with the donation. The tangible item makes the gesture feel personal, while the contribution extends its reach far beyond the holiday season.

**Q: Are charitable gifts tax-deductible in Australia?**  
A: Only organizations registered as Deductible Gift Recipients (DGRs) qualify for tax deductions. Most major mental health, environmental, and Indigenous welfare charities hold this status. Always verify on the charity’s official site or consult a registered tax agent before claiming.

**Q: How do I let someone know it’s a donation without it feeling impersonal?**  
A: Frame it as a “gift of impact.” Include a beautifully designed card or printed letter explaining why you chose that cause, what their support will fund, and how it aligns with values you share. Personalize it with a memory, a quote, or a simple note about why this matters to you both.

**Q: What if my loved one prefers traditional gifts?**  
A: Meet them where they are. You can offer a hybrid approach: a modest physical gift paired with a donation in their name, or gently suggest they “choose a cause” as their wish list alternative. Many people discover they prefer giving meaning over clutter when it’s presented with warmth and clarity.
