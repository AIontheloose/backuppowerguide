### Portfolio Construction & Risk Management Framework

Building a beginner portfolio isn't about chasing hype; it's about structural resilience. I recommend a core-satellite approach, allocating 70% of your capital to defensive, cash-generative blue-chip shares and 30% to growth-oriented or cyclical names. Diversification across sectors mitigates idiosyncratic risk. For instance, combining financials, miners, healthcare, and consumer staples ensures that a downturn in one sector won't disproportionately impact your overall returns.

#### Risk Checklist & Mitigation Strategies
| Risk Factor | Mitigation Strategy | Monitoring Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Market Volatility | Dollar-cost averaging (DCA) over 3–6 months | Monthly |
| Liquidity Constraints | Stick to ASX 200-listed stocks with >$5M daily volume | Quarterly |
| Commodity Price Swings | Hedge with non-commodity exposure (e.g., staples/tech) | Bi-annually |
| Interest Rate Sensitivity | Favour companies with low debt-to-equity (<0.5x) | Quarterly |

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the primary objective of this portfolio?**  
A: This framework targets balanced capital growth with reliable income, blending defensive consumer staples, cyclical resource exporters, established financials, healthcare innovation, and a single high-growth technology position to navigate varying economic phases while compounding wealth over time.

**Q: How often should I rebalance?**  
A: I recommend a quarterly review with annual rebalancing, or whenever any holding drifts more than 5% from its target weight. This prevents concentration risk without triggering unnecessary capital gains tax events or incurring excessive transaction costs.

**Q: Are the dividends fully franked?**  
A: CBA, WBC, BHP, and WOW distribute largely franked dividends under Australia’s imputation system, which can significantly boost after-tax returns for resident investors. CSL provides unfranked international earnings, while Xero reinvests all profits for expansion rather than distributing cash.

**Q: Is this mix suitable for beginner investors?**  
A: Yes. The combination of low-volatility staples, well-capitalised banks, and a single high-growth tech position offers a manageable entry point for long-term investors comfortable with basic ASX mechanics and dividend reinvestment plans (DRPs).

**Q: What if one holding materially underperforms?**  
A: I monitor fundamentals, not just price action. If a company’s competitive moat erodes, management guidance deteriorates, or valuation metrics detach from earnings power, I’ll trim or replace it—never out of panic, but to preserve long-term capital efficiency.
