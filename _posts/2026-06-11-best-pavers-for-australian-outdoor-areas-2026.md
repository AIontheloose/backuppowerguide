### Climate-Ready Choices & Council Variations

Australia's climate is no joke. A paver that works beautifully in the humid north might crack under Perth's heat, while a Sydney coastal home needs serious slip resistance. Beyond material choice, local council codes dictate your sub-base requirements, which can make or break your budget.

#### Material Performance by Climate
*   **Concrete:** Modern 2026 mixes include superior UV stabilisers to prevent the grey fade we used to see on balconies. Look for "pigment-through" pavers where colour runs deep, so scratches don't show.
*   **Brick:** Incredibly resilient in hot inland climates as it doesn't retain heat like dark stone. Natural variations add character to rustic or cottage-style gardens.
*   **Granite:** The gold standard for coastal areas due to its natural slip resistance and salt tolerance. It's heavier, so ensure your sub-base is robust.
*   **Composite:** Excellent for bushfire-prone zones as it won't ignite. However, check thermal expansion ratings; some composites can soften in extreme heat if not rated correctly.

#### Council Drainage & Base Requirements
Ignoring council codes is the fastest way to face fines or rework. Here's a quick reference for common requirements:

| Region / Climate | Min. Gravel Layer | Sand / Bedding Depth | Key Requirement |
|------------------|-------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Melbourne (Temperate)** | 50 mm | 25–30 mm | Permeable paving encouraged in urban zones. |
| **Perth (Hot/Dry)** | 60 mm | 30–40 mm | Dust suppression on sub-base; strict compaction specs. |
| **Brisbane/Gold Coast (Humid/Coastal)** | 50–75 mm | 25 mm | Slip resistance R11/R12 mandatory for pool surrounds. |
| **Tasmania (Cool)** | 60 mm | 40 mm | Frost-resistant concrete only; avoid porous bricks. |

> **Quick Takeaway:** Always check your local council's development plan before buying. In sensitive drainage areas, you might need permeable pavers or a French drain system, which can add $30–$50/m² to your project.

### Step-by-Step Installation Guide (Expanded)

A solid base is 90% of the battle. If you skimp here, your pavers will sink or heave within a year. Follow these steps carefully:

1.  **Mark & Excavate:** Use string lines and pegs to mark your area. Excavate to a depth of **200–250 mm** for driveways (to handle vehicle load) and **150 mm** for patios.
2.  **Geotextile Fabric:** Lay weed membrane to separate soil from aggregate, preventing mud from working its way up.
3.  **Sub-Base Layer:** Add your gravel (e.g., crushed rock). For Perth, this is 60 mm; elsewhere, 50 mm is standard. **Crucial Step:** Compact in two layers of 25 mm each. Use the plate compactor for at least 4 passes per section until no movement occurs.
4.  **Edge Forms:** Install metal edging restraints along the perimeter, anchored with spikes. This keeps your pavers locked in place and creates a clean border.
5.  **Bedding Layer:** Spread 30–40 mm of coarse sand or fine aggregate. Screed this level using pipes and a straightedge. **Do not walk on this layer!**
6.  **Lay Pavers:** Start from a corner or centre line, working outward. Leave consistent joints (usually 3–5 mm). Tap gently with a mallet to seat them.
7.  **Cutting:** Use your wet saw for precise cuts around obstacles. Measure twice, cut once!
8.  **Jointing & Final Compaction:** Sweep polymeric sand or fine aggregate into the joints. Run the compactor over the entire surface again to settle the sand and lock pavers together.
9.  **Sealing (Optional but Recommended):** For concrete and brick, apply a penetrating sealer after installation to protect against stains and UV fading.

> **Quick Takeaway:** If you're installing on a slope, ensure a fall of 1:60 away from your home for drainage. Water pooling is the enemy of any paved surface.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I mix materials in one project to score more Green Star points?**  
Yes. Layering composite edging with reclaimed timber pathways and native planting zones demonstrates material diversity and waste diversion, which assessors reward under the *Materials & Resources* category. Just ensure each product has third-party verification for recycled content or carbon footprint.

**Does efflorescence on concrete pavers ruin their warranty?**  
No. Efflorescence is a natural salt migration to the surface that doesn’t compromise structural integrity. Manufacturers exclude it from warranties because it’s harmless, temporary, and easily removed with light acid washing or rain exposure.

**How do I verify a supplier’s “sustainable” claims?**  
Demand documented proof: FSC/PEFC certification for timber, EPDs (Environmental Product Declarations) for composites, and post-consumer recycled content statements for concrete. Green Star only awards points for audited data, not marketing terminology.

**Are composite pavers slippery when wet?**  
Modern composites use brushed textures, sandblasted finishes, or aggregate blends that consistently achieve P5 slip ratings or higher. Always request the wet-rag test results before purchasing for poolside, deck, or high-moisture zones.

**Can I install these materials over existing concrete without demolition?**  
In most cases, yes. Properly prepared overlays, leveling sand beds, and interlocking systems can be laid directly over sound substrates. This approach saves landfill diversion points and reduces installation waste by up to 70% while preserving your project timeline.
