### DIY Festive Wrapping Tutorial

Wrapping doesn’t have to be a chore or a waste of money. With a little creativity, you can turn standard supplies into something truly special. Here’s my go-to 3-step method for **DIY festive wrapping**:

1️⃣ **Measure & Cut:** Lay your paper flat and cut to size, leaving an extra 5cm on each side for neat folds. If you’re using recycled brown craft paper, lightly mist it with water and let it air-dry for a subtle, vintage texture.  
2️⃣ **Fold & Secure:** Fold the long sides inward, tape them down, then tuck the short ends into triangles like an old-fashioned parcel. Use a single strip of patterned tape across the centre instead of covering every inch.  
3️⃣ **Add Natural Accents:** Thread a length of jute twine through the knot and attach a sprig of rosemary, a dried orange slice, or a pinecone foraged from your local park. It takes seconds, smells divine, and costs nothing but time.

### **FAQ: Wrapping Wisdom You’ll Actually Use**

**Q: Can I store dried botanicals and twine for next year?**  
A: Yes! Keep them in a breathable cotton pouch or cardboard box away from direct sunlight and humidity. They’ll hold their shape and scent beautifully until the next season.

**Q: What if I don’t have kraft paper or jute twine on hand?**  
A: Look around your home first. Old maps, book pages, sheet music, or even clean cereal boxes cut flat can make stunning wrapping. For ties, remove embroidery floss, use ribbon scraps, or simply fold and tuck the ends for a no-tie finish.

**Q: How do I keep heavy gifts from tearing lightweight paper?**  
A: Reinforce the bottom seam with an extra strip of tape on the inside, and place a rigid card or thin cardboard sheet underneath before folding. It adds just a few seconds but prevents disastrous splits at the checkout counter.

**Q: Is fabric wrapping really worth the learning curve?**  
A: Absolutely once you master one knot. The furoshiki-style square fold takes less than two minutes and becomes muscle memory. Plus, you’ll never have to search for tape again, and your guests will appreciate getting a usable cloth instead of trash.
