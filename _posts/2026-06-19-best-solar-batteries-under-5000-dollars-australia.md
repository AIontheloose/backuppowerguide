### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: How does depth of discharge affect my battery’s lifespan?**
Depth of discharge refers to the percentage of your battery’s total capacity that you regularly use each day. Discharging a lithium-ion cell beyond 80% daily will accelerate chemical degradation and reduce its overall cycle life. By keeping your daily drain between 60% and 75%, you can easily extend the operational lifespan of your energy storage system to ten years or more without significant capacity loss.

**Q2: Is lithium iron phosphate safer than NMC chemistry?**
Yes, lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cells are inherently more thermally stable than nickel-manganese-cobalt (NMC) variants. LFP batteries have a higher thermal runaway threshold, meaning they resist overheating and fire risks under extreme load or fault conditions. This makes them particularly well-suited for Australian climates where outdoor installations or poorly ventilated cupboards might trap heat.

**Q3: Will these batteries provide off-grid capability during blackouts?**
Most units listed here offer backup functionality when paired with a compatible hybrid inverter and transfer switch. However, true off-grid capability requires additional components like solar charge controllers and dedicated critical load panels. For standard grid-tied homes, the battery will seamlessly cover essential circuits within milliseconds of a grid failure, maintaining power for refrigeration, lighting, and communication devices.

**Q4: How long does professional installation take?**
A qualified electrician typically completes a residential battery installation in one to two days. The first day involves mounting the cabinet, routing high-voltage cabling, and configuring the battery management system. The second day focuses on commissioning, grid compliance testing, and app configuration. Always ensure your installer holds a current AER accreditation and follows AS/NZS 5139 safety standards.
