### Seal the Gaps Before You Heat

What I've found is that many families jump straight to buying heaters before checking their doors and windows. It's a bit like trying to fill a bucket with a hole in the bottom! Small openings around frames can account for up to 20% of heat loss if left unsealed. In my kitchen, a simple door-frame seal transformed the warmth overnight. Start by auditing your home on a windy day; feel for those sneaky drafts near skirting boards and windows. Once you've spotted them, tackle these essentials:

*   **Draft Excluders:** Fantastic for external doors. Look for sturdy fabrics or faux fur that kids love. Price point is usually around $12 per unit, making them a budget-friendly win.
*   **Weather Stripping Kit:** Your best mate for window and door frames. At roughly $35, a complete kit with adhesive, foam, and trim covers all bases to stop those chilly breezes from sneaking in.

> **Pro Tip:** Seal 95% of drafts using draft excluders on all external doors; each unit costs ~$12, but the payoff is huge—saving up to $120 in heating energy annually based on average savings! When searching for a **draft excluder Australia**-wide supplier, prioritise durable materials that won't sag by spring. ### Boost Your Thermal Performance with Insulation and Film

Once your doors are sealed, it's time to tackle the roof and windows before you hit the Christmas party prep. For practical **Australian winter insulation tips**, focus on the attic and glazing first. Adding rigid foam board to the attic is a game-changer for homes in Victoria and South Australia where winters demand robust heating. A 25 mm sheet covers 1 m² for just $25. While this might sound technical, think of it as giving your roof a thick woolly jumper. Insulating a typical 30 m² attic space (~$750 total) can slash your heating bills by up to 15%. It's an investment that pays you back every winter.

For those in QLD or coastal NSW, the climate is milder, but draught reduction still matters. **Window insulation film** is perfect here. Priced at $45 for a set covering four standard windows, it's a DIY hero. Install this during peak months of June and July to trap heat inside without blocking the view. It's invisible, safe, and incredibly effective at reducing heat loss.

### Claiming Your 2026 Rebate

Let's talk about that $500 rebate. How do you claim it? In 2026, the process is streamlined. To access **energy efficiency rebates Australia** offers under the National Energy Efficiency Target, you'll need to submit a certified energy audit report through the Australian Energy Regulator portal. Eligibility criteria have been updated; your home must be built before 2015 to qualify for the retrofit tier, and the work must demonstrate a minimum 10% improvement in thermal performance. Keep receipts and photos of the installation for verification. This **home retrofits rebate** isn't just about saving cash now; it's about boosting your property value and comfort for years to come.
