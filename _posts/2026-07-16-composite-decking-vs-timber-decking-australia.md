### The Real Cost Breakdown: What Your Wallet Actually Sees

Let’s address the elephant in the room straight away: that initial checkout price will absolutely give you pause. In 2026, composite decking still carries a premium upfront, but the gap has narrowed considerably thanks to increased local manufacturing and competitive supply chains. Quality composite boards now average **$110–120 AUD/m²**, while standard-grade timber like Western Red Cedar or Tasmanian Oak sits comfortably between **$70–90 AUD/m²**. High-grade spotted gum or Jarrah will push closer to **$115 AUD/m²**, so don’t let a single outdated figure fool you.

**Pro Tip:** If you’re leaning towards timber for that authentic Aussie warmth, look into the *Green Homes Initiative*. In NSW and QLD, you can claim a **$200 rebate**, while ACT residents qualify for **$300**. To actually get it paid out, keep your FSC certification documents handy, submit the claim form within 60 days of material delivery, and attach your supplier’s tax invoice. Processing usually takes 10–14 business days, but missing that paperwork window is the most common reason homeowners miss out.

When you weigh the sticker price against total cost of ownership, timber looks cheaper initially but demands consistent financial upkeep. Composite asks for more upfront but shields you from recurring sealant costs and premature board replacements. Both paths are valid; it just comes down to whether you’d rather spend your money now or your time later.

### Maintenance & Lifespan: Trading Weekends for Peace of Mind

I completely understand the frustration of watching a beautiful deck slowly lose its luster while you’re trying to host a weekend barbie. Here’s where the long-term picture becomes crystal clear.

**Composite decking** is engineered for low-maintenance living. Modern capped polymer blends require only a thorough hose-down and soft-bristle brush **once every five years** to maintain their appearance. Daily dust and pollen wipe away easily, but the heavy lifting stays rare. You’re looking at a realistic lifespan of **30–40 years**, with most reputable brands now offering warranties that extend to 25–30 years—though keep in mind that many Australian suppliers require you to log basic maintenance checks online to keep the coverage valid.

**Timber decking** remains a labour of love that rewards attention. Without consistent care, expect **10–15 years** before noticeable wear sets in. You’ll need to apply a quality sealant **quarterly**, which runs roughly **$25–35 AUD per 20 m²** per coat. That might sound modest until you tally four applications annually over a decade. Timbers typically carry shorter warranties of **3–5 years** because natural grain variation and climate exposure make uniform performance harder to guarantee. 

If you value uninterrupted weekends and hate the thought of ladder-climbing in February heat, composite is genuinely hard to beat. If you find sanding and staining deeply therapeutic, or you simply love that fresh-cut timber scent after a summer rain, a well-tended deck can absolutely last for decades. Just remember: skipping that quarterly seal invites moisture ingress, which accelerates warping far faster than most homeowners anticipate under our intense UV exposure.

### Climate, Codes & The Heat Factor

Building outdoors in Australia isn’t like building elsewhere. Our climate zones dictate material behaviour more than anything else.

**Heat Retention Reality:** Composite boards do absorb and retain heat more than timber. Thermal conductivity tests in 2026 show that dark-coloured composites can reach surface temperatures of **55–65°C** under direct sun, while lighter shades (sandy, grey, or pale oak tones) stay closer to **40–45°C**. Timber naturally dissipates heat faster due to its porous structure. If barefoot entertaining is non-negotiable, stick to light-coloured composites or opt for timber with a matte finish.

**State-Specific Building Codes:** Compliance isn’t optional in 2026. NSW’s *Residential Construction Code* strictly mandates a minimum **30 mm board spacing** on composite decks to allow cross-ventilation and prevent thermal expansion buckling. Victoria requires similar gaps but emphasises joist spacing at **450 mm centres** for load distribution, while QLD focuses heavily on termite-resistant subframes near the coast. Always verify your local council’s development application requirements before laying your first sleeper.

**Environmental Trade-Offs:** Sustainability matters more than ever. Quality composites now utilise up to **50% recycled plastic**, keeping millions of kilograms out of landfill annually. Timber, when sourced FSC-certified, offers complete renewability and natural carbon sequestration. Neither option is flawless, but both have evolved into responsible choices depending on your priority: waste diversion versus sustainable forestry.

### Installation Realities & DIY Considerations

About **62%** of Australian homeowners attempt decking projects themselves, which is wonderful—but it’s also where many hit unexpected roadblocks. Composite installation relies heavily on hidden clip systems or interlocking grooves that demand precise spacing. A single misaligned spacer can cause board gaps to widen as temperatures shift. Timber, meanwhile, rewards precision jointing but requires careful pre-drilling to prevent splitting and demands exact moisture content matching between joists and boards.

When measuring your footprint, always add a **10 cm margin** for joist overlap and edge cuts. I’ve watched too many capable DIYers run short mid-project, forcing costly second deliveries or awkward last-minute cuts that compromise structural integrity. ### Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Which decking material actually performs better in coastal humidity and salt spray?**
Composite decking is specifically engineered to resist moisture absorption and marine borers, making it a far more reliable choice for properties within 500 metres of the coastline. Timber, even when treated with preservatives, will gradually degrade if exposed to consistent salt wind without rigorous sealing and ventilation. If you live near the coast and want minimal annual upkeep, composite is genuinely the smarter long-term pick.

**2. How do I actually claim the Green Homes rebate for timber decking?**
You’ll need to gather your FSC certification documents, the supplier’s detailed tax invoice, and a completed state-specific claim form from the official government portal. Submit everything within 60 days of purchasing your materials, and keep digital copies of all correspondence for reference. Processing typically takes 10–14 business days, but delays often occur when suppliers forget to include mandatory eco-sourcing declarations on their invoices.

**3. Can I safely install composite decking myself in a hot climate without it becoming too hot to walk on?**
Yes, provided you choose lighter colour tones and maintain the manufacturer’s recommended ventilation gaps. Dark composites absorb significantly more solar radiation, but sandy, grey, or pale oak finishes stay considerably cooler underfoot. You can also apply a light-coloured UV-protective spray in summer months if your local supplier offers them, which helps reflect heat without voiding standard warranty terms.

**4. What’s the most common mistake DIYers make when comparing composite and timber?**
People usually compare only the upfront material cost while ignoring total ownership expenses over ten years. Timber looks cheaper at checkout but accumulates sealant costs, occasional board replacements, and lost weekend labour. Composite demands more capital initially but shields you from recurring maintenance fees and premature degradation. Always calculate a full decade of projected expenses before committing to either option.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does composite decking actually last compared to timber?**  
Quality composite boards typically carry 25- to 30-year manufacturer warranties and perform reliably for 15–20 years with minimal upkeep. Well-maintained hardwoods like spotted gum, merbau, or blackbutt can last 20–30+ years, but only if you commit to annual cleaning, sanding, and resealing.

**Is composite decking safe in hot climates?**  
Dark composites can absorb and retain heat, sometimes becoming uncomfortably warm underfoot. Choose lighter shades, textured slip-resistant surfaces, and ensure your subfloor has adequate airflow. Timber naturally dissipates heat faster but may check or splinter if left unsealed.

**Which material is more environmentally sustainable?**  
Composite decking diverts plastic waste and wood fibers from landfills, though end-of-life recycling remains limited. Timber is fully biodegradable and renewable when sourced from FSC-certified forests. If you prioritize low embodied carbon and are willing to maintain it, timber wins on eco-credentials; composite offers a lower-carbon footprint only if you skip refinishing cycles entirely.

**Can I install either material myself?**  
Composite systems often feature hidden clip fasteners and interlocking edges that simplify DIY installation. Timber demands precise cutting, corrosion-resistant fixings, and careful joint sealing, making professional installation highly advisable unless you have proven carpentry experience.
