### My Step-by-Step Sunday Workflow

Meal prep doesn’t mean surrendering your entire weekend to the kitchen. I’ve streamlined a realistic three-hour window that fits even the tightest schedules:

1. **Shop Smart (30 mins):** Stick to a list of 8–10 core items. Avoid the centre aisles where impulse buys live. Grab your proteins, base carbs, seasonal veg, and pantry staples first.
2. **Active Prep (90 mins):** Set a timer. Roast proteins and root vegetables simultaneously on sheet pans. Boil rice or quinoa in an instant pot. Wash and chop raw veggies for crunch. Wipe down surfaces as you go—kitchen flow matters.
3. **Portion & Store (60 mins):** Use a digital scale to portion meals uniformly. Label every container with the date and contents. Stack glass containers vertically to maximise fridge real estate.

This **batch cooking benefits** approach reduces daily decision fatigue and ensures you always have nourishing food within reach. ### Healthy Grab-and-Go Meals & Seasonal Swaps

Rotating ingredients prevents palate fatigue and keeps grocery bills low. Here are two anchor recipes I swear by:

**1. Sheet-Pan Chicken & Sweet Potato Bowls**  
Toss 500g chicken strips, 2 diced sweet potatoes, and 1 chopped capsicum in olive oil, smoked paprika, and garlic powder. Roast at 200°C for 25 mins. Serve over microwavable brown rice with a squeeze of lemon. *Seasonal swap:* In late summer, replace capsicum with courgette or tinned tomatoes for moisture and fibre.

**2. Quinoa & Tinned Fish Power Bowls**  
Mix cooked quinoa with rinsed tinned salmon or sardines, chopped parsley, cucumber ribbons, and a yoghurt-dill dressing. *Seasonal swap:* Swap winter greens for spring spinach or edible flowers when local markets stock them cheaply.

These **healthy grab-and-go meals** travel beautifully in insulated containers and maintain texture even after reheating. When drought hits, lean into root vegetables, tinned legumes, and hardy brassicas—they store longer and nutritionally hold their ground.

### Storage, Safety & Freezer Meal Prep Hacks

Proper storage extends your prep window from days to months while preserving nutrient density. I recommend BPA-free glass containers for microwave reheating and silicone bags for freezer stacking. Label everything with a waterproof marker: meal name, date prepared, and reheating instructions. Cooked poultry and seafood keep safely in the fridge for 3–4 days; freeze portions within 24 hours of cooking if you won’t eat them by day three. When reheating, ensure the core temperature reaches 75°C to eliminate bacterial risk. Thaw frozen meals overnight in the fridge or use the defrost setting on your microwave—never at room temperature. ### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long will my prepped meals stay safe to eat?**  
A: Properly stored in airtight containers, most cooked meals last 3–4 days in the fridge and up to 3 months in the freezer. Always trust your senses—if it smells off, has an unusual texture, or looks discolored, discard it immediately.

**Q: Can I reheat prepped food more than once?**  
A: No. Each reheating cycle breaks down nutrients, alters texture, and increases bacterial risk. Reheat only what you plan to eat that day, and keep the rest chilled until your next meal window.

**Q: Do glass containers work better than plastic for freezing?**  
A: Generally, yes. Glass won’t absorb stains or odors, survives temperature swings better, and is microwave-safe without leaching chemicals. Just ensure they’re labeled “freezer-to-oven” safe to prevent cracking during rapid temperature changes.

**Q: How do I stop my prepped meals from getting soggy?**  
A: Store dressings, sauces, and wet ingredients separately in small divider containers. Assemble or drizzle just before eating. For salads, layer hardy greens at the bottom and keep dressings on top to avoid wilting.
