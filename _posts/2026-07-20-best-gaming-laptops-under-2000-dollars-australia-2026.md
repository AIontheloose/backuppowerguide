### Why the $2k AUD Threshold Still Dictates Value
In today’s market, $2,000 AUD is the absolute ceiling where you secure a modern **RTX 4060** class GPU with full power output, rather than a heavily downclocked mobile variant. Step below that and you’re usually forced into DDR4 memory or weak cooling solutions that invite **thermal throttling** within minutes of launching a demanding title. Above it, you pay for chassis materials and brand prestige rather than measurable performance gains. For the average Aussie student, casual shooter, or part-time streamer, this price bracket delivers the most realistic 1080p/1440p gaming experience without financial regret.

MSI has finally dropped the Raider naming fatigue and doubled down on thermal architecture. That’s impressive for a mid-range chassis, though you’ll need to run the fans on ‘Turbo’ to keep boost clocks stable. ### FAQ

**Q: Can I upgrade the RAM and storage on the MSI Katana 15?**  
A: Yes. The laptop ships with 16GB DDR5 soldered to one stick and leaves one SO-DIMM slot open, allowing you to add up to 64GB total. The single M.2 NVMe slot is easily accessible via the bottom panel, though you’ll need a second drive bay or external storage for expansion.

**Q: How does the Katana 15 stack up against the Dell G15 5530?**  
A: The Katana offers better sustained boost clocks and slightly more aggressive cooling, but the Dell G15 includes Thunderbolt 4, a Kensington lock, and ProSupport—making it the stronger choice for hybrid work/study use cases.

**Q: Is thermal throttling at 82°C a problem?**  
A: Not necessarily. Modern RTX 4060 laptops are designed to throttle around 80–85°C to preserve silicon longevity. As long as frame times remain stable (which they do in our testing), you’re getting exactly the performance Dell and MSI engineered for this chassis.

**Q: Should I use it for video editing or streaming?**  
A: Absolutely. The Ryzen 7 7840H’s 8-core/16-thread architecture handles Premiere Pro and OBS smoothly, while the RTX 4060 accelerates encoding via NVENC. Just ensure you’re running on AC power to avoid CPU/GPU power limit restrictions.

### Conclusion

After weeks of stress-testing thermals, benchmarking frame pacing, and weighing real-world value, the MSI Katana 15 B13V emerges as a compelling option for gamers who prioritize raw performance over premium build materials. If you’re chasing consistent 1080p/1440p gaming on a budget and don’t mind managing fan curves, this chassis delivers remarkable price-to-performance ratios. However, if your workflow blends gaming with professional tasks, the Dell G15’s Thunderbolt support and enterprise-grade warranty make it the safer long-term investment.
